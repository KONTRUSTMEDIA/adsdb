    <p>
      The
      <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zero-width_space" target="_blank">zero-width space</a>
      character does not indicate a visible separation. Without hyphenating a word zero-width space can break it to a new
      line.
    </p>
    <p>
      Without visible space between two words, this character shows the end of one word and the start of another.
    </p>
  </section>
  <section id="space">
    <h3>Space</h3>
    <p>
      Space is the common character that is usually present in between two words. This is the same character that is generated
      by pressing the space bar.
    </p>
  </section>
  <section id="no-break">
    <h3>No-Break Space</h3>
    <p>No-break space (NBSP) adds a space between two words without breaking the line.</p>
  </section>
  <section id="en-space">
    <h3>En Space & Em Space</h3>
    <p>En is the width of the capital alphabet "N". It is equal to half of the width of the em.</p>
    <p>
      On the other hand, Em space is equal to the width of the capital "M". It is equivalent to the currently specified font
      size.
    </p>
  </section>
  <section>
    <h3>Three-per-em & Four-per-em Space</h3>
    <p>Three-per-em is also known as Thick space. It is one-third of an em.</p>
    <p>Four-per-em is also known as Mid space. It is one-fourth of an em.</p>
  </section>
  <section id="six-per-em">
    <h3>Six-per-em Space</h3>
    <p>
      It is one-sixth of an em. Use it to add a little space between the two characters. It is similar to the Thin
      space.
    </p>
  </section>
  <section id="figure">
    <h3>Figure Space</h3>
    <p>Its width is equal to the size of a digit. It is preferably used in numerals.</p>
  </section>
  <section id="punctuation">
    <h3>Punctuation Space</h3>
    <p>
      Its size is equal to the width of a period. To add whitespace before a punctuation mark, punctuation space can be
      utilized.
    </p>
  </section>
  <section id="thin">
    <h3>Thin Space</h3>
    <p>Just like its name, this space is thin. It is one-fifth or can be one-sixth of an em.</p>
  </section>
  <section id="hair">
    <h3>Hair Space</h3>
    <p>
      The width of the hair space is even smaller than the thin space. It can be used to place a little blank space between
      two characters.
    </p>
  </section>

    <h3>Narrow No-Break Space</h3>
    <p>It works like No-Break Space.</p>
  </section>

    <p>Medium Mathematical Space(MMSP) is used in Mathematical formulas.</p>
