<h2><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>How Much is Safe for my Large Breed Puppy?</strong></span></h2>
<p>Just like with humans, bigger isn&#x2019;t always better for canines. Dogs need to be in their optimal weight zone. To achieve this, they need to be fed a healthy diet, and exercised regularly. <p>The bones and joints take time to grow and forge correctly. Rapid growth can cause arthritis, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hip_dysplasia_%28canine%29">hip dysplasia</a> and knee joint problems later in life. This will be painful for your dog, and expensive for you. Your dog&#x2019;s skeleton <strong>isn&#x2019;t fully developed until your dog is two years old</strong>. <p>Large and giant puppies grow faster than their smaller counterparts, and they stay at the puppy stage for longer than small dogs too.
