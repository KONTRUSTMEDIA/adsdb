                <h2>Why I Made This Infinite Fusion Calculator?</h2>
                <hr>
                <p>I created this tool because I love playing the Infinite Fusion
                        Game.</a> I noticed that existing Infinite
                    Fusion Calculators lacked the latest data and had outdated designs. With the latest v100 sprite
                    pack, including 178,060 custom sprites, I updated this Infinite Fusion Calculator to provide
                    trainers with the most up-to-date information. Unlike other calculators, this tool features a modern
                    design, responsive functionality across all devices, and is open source. Additionally, while other
                    calculators may not have the v6.7 data and rely on in-browser calculations, this tool ensures fast
                    and
                    accurate calculations by utilizing powerful server-side processes.</p>
                <h2>How To Use Infinite Fusion Calculator</h2>
                <hr>
                <p>Enter the names or Dex IDs of two Pokémon into the respective input fields.</p>
                <p>Click the "Fuse" button to calculate the fusion of the chosen Pokémon.</p>
                <p>To fuse random Pokémon, click the "Random" button.</p>
                <p>Click the "Reset" button to clear the inputs and reset the page to its default state.</p>
                <!-- <div class="line"></div> -->
                <h2>How Infinite Fusion Calculator Works?</h2>
                <hr>
                <p>The Pokemon Infinite Fusion Calculator is a powerful tool designed to assist trainers in creating
                    unique fusion combinations of Pokemon.</p>
                <p>I have updated all the data of Pokemon up to version 6.7. We now have a total of 501 Pokemon, and all
                    errors have been fixed. Now, you can calculate infinite fusions with the latest Pokemon.</p>
                <p>When you enter the names of the Pokemon and click the calculate button, we retrieve the Pokedex IDs
                    of both Pokemon. Using these Pokedex IDs, we access the database to retrieve images, stats, and
                    various other calculation data.</p>
                <p>This tool displays both Pokemon's custom sprites and autogen sprites along with their Pokedex IDs.
                    Below the images, you will find other Pokemon's alternative custom sprites Variants, stats, Pokemon
                    types, and type effectiveness data against your chosen Pokemon's types.</p>
                <h2>New Feature: Variants</h2>
                <hr>
                <p style="font-style: italic;">The latest update to the Infinite Fusion Calculator introduces the
                    "Variants" feature, allowing trainers to explore alternative forms and variations of fused Pokemon.
                    With this enhancement, trainers can now view all available variants of a fused Pokemon, providing a
                    comprehensive look at the different fusion options available. This feature adds depth to the fusion
                    experience, enabling trainers to discover unique and exciting fusion combinations.</p>

                <p>Here are some additional details and tips:</p>
                <ul>
                    <li>This game uses assets from Gen 5, but Pokemon Learnsets are based on their Gen 7 iteration. You
                        can find Gen 7 information on Bulbapedia or Serebii.</li>
                    <li>The Pokeradar is an item that can be used for shiny hunting and finding hidden Pokemon in grass.
                    </li>
                    <li>Teleport replaces Fly as an early game HM.</li>
                    <li>You can enable Debug mode for various benefits, including giving yourself infinite items and
                        Pokemon.</li>
                    <li>Many Pokemon can be combined with themselves and have custom sprites.</li>
                </ul>

                <h2>Simple Fusion Ideas</h2>
                <p>Here are some simple fusion ideas:</p>
                <ul>
                    <li>Early Eevee can be used for Adaptability on any mon.</li>
                    <li>Bidoof/Bibarel provide Simple to double stat changes.</li>
                    <li>Koffing/Weezing combined with a Dark Type create a Pokemon with no weaknesses.</li>
                    <li>Slaking and Shedinja create powerful fusions.</li>
                    <li>An early Azurill/Marill can provide Huge Power to any Pokemon.</li>
                    <li>Parasect combined with a Steel type gives either a Steel/Bug or Steel/Grass Pokemon.</li>
                    <li>Joltik with Compound Eyes and a Mon with Fury Cutter or Rollout allows for easy sweeps.</li>
                    <li>Zubat/Golbat and Gyarados create strong early to mid-game fusions.</li>
                </ul>

                <ol>
                    <li><strong>Normal</strong>: This type is neutral against all other types.</li>
                    <li><strong>Fire</strong>: Effective against Grass, Bug, Ice, and Steel types, but weak against
                        Water, Rock, and Fire types.</li>
                    <li><strong>Water</strong>: Strong against Fire, Ground, and Rock types, but vulnerable to Grass and
                        Electric types.</li>
                    <li><strong>Electric</strong>: Effective against Water and Flying types, but weak against Ground
                        types.</li>
                    <li><strong>Grass</strong>: Strong against Water, Ground, and Rock types, but vulnerable to Fire,
                        Grass, Poison, Flying, Bug, and Ice types.</li>
                    <li><strong>Ice</strong>: Effective against Grass, Ground, Flying, and Dragon types, but weak
                        against Fire, Water, Ice, and Steel types.</li>
                    <li><strong>Fighting</strong>: Strong against Normal, Ice, Rock, Dark, and Steel types, but weak
                        against Flying, Poison, Psychic, Bug, Ghost, and Fairy types.</li>
                    <li><strong>Poison</strong>: Effective against Grass and Fairy types, but weak against Poison,
                        Ground, Rock, Ghost, and Steel types.</li>
                    <li><strong>Ground</strong>: Strong against Fire, Electric, Poison, Rock, and Steel types, but
                        vulnerable to Water, Grass, and Ice types.</li>
                    <li><strong>Flying</strong>: Effective against Grass, Fighting, and Bug types, but weak against
                        Electric, Rock, and Steel types.</li>
                    <li><strong>Psychic</strong>: Strong against Fighting and Poison types, but weak against Psychic and
                        Steel types.</li>
                    <li><strong>Bug</strong>: Effective against Grass, Psychic, and Dark types, but weak against Fire,
                        Fighting, Flying, Poison, Ghost, Steel, and Fairy types.</li>

                        Normal types.</li>

                        Dark, and Fairy types.</li>

                        Water, Electric, and Steel types.</li>
                    <li><strong>Fairy</strong>: Effective against Fighting, Dragon, and Dark types, but weak against
                        Fire, Poison, and Steel types.</li>
