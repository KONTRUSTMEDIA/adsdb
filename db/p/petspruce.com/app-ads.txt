What is truly unique about the Echidna is that it lives in such a small area that is only a few square miles. It also is one of the only animals that has four limbs and is able to walk on two legs. It can only be found in Australia.</em></strong></p><p>The next time you ask a person what the true rarity of the Echidna is, it is very likely that they will say that it is rare because of the amazing ability of the Echidna to live in such a small area. </p><p>People that see the Echidna often question if the animal is truly rare.</p><p>The answer to this question would depend on the number of people that see the animal, or if the animal was ever seen outside of Australia.</p><h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>How do Echidnas reproduce?</strong></h2><p>The reproductive system of an Echidna (another name for an Elephant Bird) is unique among mammals.</p><p>The male of the species has one pair of erectile organs &ndash; penises &ndash; that are attached to his mid-section, while the female has two, one inside and one outside her body. She releases eggs (or sperm) that are in a sac, which is located near her anus.</p><p>The eggs are carried by the sperm to the oviducts, which is the tube through which an egg moves from the ovary to the uterus.</p><p>Inside the oviduct, the developing embryo attaches itself to a receptor on the surface of the uterine wall. </p><p>There, it becomes nourished by the lining and by hormone secreted by the mother to protect it.</p><p>After nine months of development, the baby Echidna can then exit the uterus and is completely independent from its mother.</p><p>However, if the mother is not receptive to the child, she may be unable to care for it. </p><p>Otherwise, it will remain within the mother&rsquo;s body as it matures.</p><p>It may take up to 20 years for an Echidna to mature from an egg to a fully-grown adult. </p><p>One of the main factors for this process is the size of the parents. </p><p>Echidnas that grow to a mature size of 1.6 feet are believed to be capable of reproduction.</p><p>The primary difference between how the Echidna&rsquo;s reproductive system works is that there is no pregnancy. Baby Echidnas grows very slowly. This can result in delayed feeding.</p><p>The baby Echidna may eat, but it may only consume a few seconds before it is born.</p><p>Mother and child bonding is an important aspect of Echidna culture. To increase the chances of raising a baby Echidna, all potential mothers are treated gently and humanely.</p><p>A mother will not attack her baby if it is attacked, even if it is hurt. However, this does not mean that any harm done to the baby is acceptable.</p><p>The reproductive pattern of Echidnas has long been a mystery to many scientists, who may still debate on the matter.</p><h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Conclusion</strong></h3><p>Regardless of how Echidnas reproduce, they are, first and foremost, great pets. </p><p>They have such complex and intricate social systems that they make wonderful study subjects for behavioral ecologists.</p>			</div>
