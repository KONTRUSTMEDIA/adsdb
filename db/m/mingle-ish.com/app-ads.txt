			<h2 style="text-align: center;">phrasal verbs (verb + preposition)</h2>
<p style="text-align: center;">The dreaded phrasal verbs have reared their ugly head! If you&#8217;ve tried to bury your head in the sand and avoid these, now is the time to learn.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;">If you don&#8217;t know what a phrasal verb is, I suggest you find out, pronto. They are a huge part of the English language and are used frequently amongst native English speakers. The annoying part about phrasal verbs (for English learners) is that they don&#8217;t make sense.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;">We take a verb and add a preposition (or two) to the end of it. This makes a well-known phrase that only native speakers understand. Like a secret code. The verb and preposition can mean totally different things but when joined mean something.</p>

			<h2 style="text-align: center;">pronunciation</h2>
<p style="text-align: center;" align="center">So, you&#8217;ve learnt grammar, you’re getting good at communicating in English, what&#8217;s the next logical step? To become a pro of course! How do you become a pro? By nailing pronunciation of course.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;" align="center">Before we begin, I&#8217;m going to give you a few BIG tips about English pronunciation:</p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Words are not necessarily pronounced how they look.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Different letters have multiple sounds.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Some letters are silent.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;">We have a lot of French influence.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;">There can be slight differences in vowel sounds which can cause confusion.
