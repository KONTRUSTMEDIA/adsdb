                <div class="sync-about-text">
                    <h3>What is Clock Synchronization?</h3>
                    <p>Your device's clock is never perfectly aligned with the true, official time. The "clock offset"
                        shows this tiny difference, measured in milliseconds. This feature compares your device's time
                        to our high-precision servers, which are synchronized with global time standards, similar to
                        atomic clocks.</p>

                    <h3>How We Calculate the Offset</h3>
                    <p>Our servers use the <strong>Network Time Protocol (NTP)</strong> to stay precisely aligned with
                        <strong>Coordinated Universal Time (UTC)</strong>. When you open this page, the system
                        automatically detects your timezone and starts the synchronization process.
                    </p>
                    <p>Immediately after, we measure the <strong>round-trip time</strong> it takes for data to travel
                        from your browser to our server and back. We account for this network travel time to correct for
                        any delay. By comparing the adjusted server timestamp against your device, we can calculate the
                        true offset of your clock with high precision. This entire process takes just a few milliseconds
                        to complete.</p>
                    <p>If your device time is lower than the true server time, it means your clock is <strong>running
                            behind</strong>. If your device time is higher than the server time, your clock is
                        <strong>running ahead</strong>.
                    </p>

                    <h3>What is Network Precision?</h3>
                    <p>When you visit our clock page, MSTimer connects to our dedicated time server to retrieve the
                        data. This round trip takes a small amount of time to complete, typically ranging from a few
                        milliseconds to a second. The exact speed depends on several factors, including your internet
                        connection and the server's response time.</p>
                    <p><strong>Network Precision</strong> represents that tiny delay. It acts as a margin of error,
                        telling you exactly how close the time on your screen is to the official atomic time. A
                        <strong>lower number</strong> indicates a stronger connection and higher accuracy.
                    </p>

                    <h3>Is My Clock Accurate?</h3>
                    <p>Whether your device is a few milliseconds (ms) ahead or behind, you can consider your clock
                        <strong>accurate to the second</strong>. This tiny difference is imperceptible to humans,
                        meaning your device is perfectly synchronized for all practical daily needs.
                    </p>
                    <p><em>Note: If you manually adjust your device time, please allow up to 25 seconds for the clock to
                            detect the change and re-sync automatically. You can also reload the page or use the "Sync
                            Now" button for an instant update.</em></p>
                </div>
