</header><div class="entry-content"><p><img decoding="async" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-364" src="https://www.magnet-fishing.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/04/Depositphotos_224470546_l-2015-1024x684.jpg" alt="magnet fishing people throwing magnet in river" width="610" height="407" srcset="https://www.magnet-fishing.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/04/Depositphotos_224470546_l-2015-1024x684.jpg 1024w, https://www.magnet-fishing.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/04/Depositphotos_224470546_l-2015-300x200.jpg 300w, https://www.magnet-fishing.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/04/Depositphotos_224470546_l-2015-768x513.jpg 768w, https://www.magnet-fishing.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/04/Depositphotos_224470546_l-2015.jpg 2000w" sizes="(max-width: 610px) 100vw, 610px" /></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Magnet fishing</strong> makes use of a <strong>strong magnet</strong> to <strong>recover magnetic objects</strong> from water.</li>
<li>The magnet has a loop or eye attached to it.</li>
<li>A rope is tied to the loop or eye in order to recover the magnet and the objects that attach themselves to it. It is not suitable for items that are not attracted to a magnet such as gold.</li>
<li>Not only is it very useful to recover lost items but is also rapidly becoming a favorite pastime/hobby for hundreds of people worldwide.</li>
<li>Magnets come in different weights and sizes and are measured in diameter (mm) and pull force (Llbs or Kg).</li>
</ul>
<h2></h2>
<h2>Equipment Needed For Magnet Fishing</h2>
<p><img decoding="async" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-254" src="https://www.magnet-fishing.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/fishingrope-300x201.jpg" alt="ropes for magnet fishing" width="300" height="201" srcset="https://www.magnet-fishing.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/fishingrope-300x201.jpg 300w, https://www.magnet-fishing.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/fishingrope-768x515.jpg 768w, https://www.magnet-fishing.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/fishingrope-1024x687.jpg 1024w, https://www.magnet-fishing.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/fishingrope.jpg 2000w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" />In short, you will need:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>A really <a href="https://www.magnet-fishing.com/best-magnet-magnet-fishing/">good strong magnet</a></strong></li>
<li><strong>A <a href="https://www.magnet-fishing.com/best-rope-magnet-fishing/">sturdy rope</a></strong></li>
<li><strong>Gloves</strong></li>
<li><strong>Somewhere to put place your finds such as a bucket</strong></li>
<li><strong>A way to clean your hands e.g. hand sanitizer</strong></li>
</ul>
<p><strong>(See our recommended Magnet Fishing Kit below to get started right away)</strong></p>
<h3><strong>The Magnet</strong></h3>
<p>Your magnet needs an eyebolt for threading your rope through. We suggest you invest in a Neodymium Magnet. You can find our <a href="https://www.magnet-fishing.com/best-magnet-magnet-fishing/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Top 10 recommended magnets here</a>.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><strong>The Rope</strong></h3>
<p>A Paracord or Nylon rope is highly recommended. We&#8217;ve reviewed some ropes <a href="https://www.magnet-fishing.com/best-rope-magnet-fishing/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">here</a>.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><strong>Gloves</strong></h3>
<p>Gloves will not only protect your hands from the much and dirt involved with dragging items from a lake but they will also stop you from getting any injuries from the sharp metallic objects that you will be pulling out.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><b>Somewhere To Keep Your Magnet Fishing Finds</b></h3>
<p>Goes without saying, keep your stuff in one place so you can take it away with you otherwise you will be lugging stuff home in your pockets.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><b>A Way to Keep Your Hands Clean</b></h3>
<p>You don&#8217;t want the dirt from the lake covering your hands in germs, be sensible and take hand sanitizer and/or a cloth to wipe your hands upon.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><strong>Threadlock (optional)</strong></h3>
<p>The Threadlock is optional but recommended by the more experienced Magnet Fishers as a way of preventing the rope being lost. It provides a way to secure the rope in the eyebolt without you having to keep a tight grip on it all of the time.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><b>Knife</b></h3>
<p>This is an optional item that you can use to cut your rope free if it get&#8217;s tangled onto anything in the lake (and it does happen!) otherwise you&#8217;ll end up losing the rope and/or the magnet and they&#8217;re not necessarily a cheap item!</p>
<p>You can find a more detailed article on our recommended <a href="https://www.magnet-fishing.com/how-to-magnet-fish/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">magnet fishing accessories here</a>.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>

<h2>How to Magnet Fish</h2>
<p><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-252" src="https://www.magnet-fishing.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/magnetpic-200x300.jpg" alt="how to magnet fish" width="200" height="300" srcset="https://www.magnet-fishing.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/magnetpic-200x300.jpg 200w, https://www.magnet-fishing.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/magnetpic-768x1151.jpg 768w, https://www.magnet-fishing.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/magnetpic-684x1024.jpg 684w, https://www.magnet-fishing.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/magnetpic.jpg 1335w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 200px) 100vw, 200px" />We have a dedicated guide for <a href="https://www.magnet-fishing.com/how-to-magnet-fish/">how to magnet fish</a> but it doesn&#8217;t hurt to also include a quick overview here too.</p>
<p>The techniques for Magnet Fishing are incredibly simple, in fact, there&#8217;s hardly any skill required at.</p>
<p>But for those people who are curious about how it all works at this point, you may have no idea how to do it!</p>
<p>So here&#8217;s a couple of tried and tested methods:</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><strong>The Throw-In &amp; Pull Back Technique</strong></h3>
<p>As it sounds. With your magnet tied securely to your rope, you simply throw the line into the body of water and <strong>slowly</strong> pull the line back in again.</p>
<p>Don&#8217;t pull it too quickly as the magnet could snag onto something solid and get lodged, you also face the risk of losing your treasure if the magnet passes too quickly over the items in the water.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><strong>The Throw-In, Walk &amp; Pull Back Technique</strong></h3>
<p>Again, this is how it sounds. You launch your line into the body of water and instead of simply pulling it back in you walk along the river bank or canal bank to give the magnet more area to cover.</p>
<p>You, therefore, increase the chances of attracting more finds.</p>
