<p dir="ltr"><span>2026 Defensive Awareness Clinic</span></p>
<p dir="ltr"><span>&nbsp;</span></p>
<p dir="ltr"><span>This defensive-minded specialty program will be offered to each player, </span><span>defenseman and forwards. </span><span>&nbsp;</span></p>
<p dir="ltr"><span>Much attention is placed on offensive skills as well since defensemen are a big part of team’s offence.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p dir="ltr"><span>Each player will be taught what to do without the puck, with the puck, and how gain possession of the&nbsp;</span></p>
<p dir="ltr"><span>puck. Every drill at the clinic will be performed at game speed and all the players will be pushed out of&nbsp;</span></p>
<p dir="ltr"><span>their comfort zone.</span></p>
<p dir="ltr"><span>&nbsp;</span></p>
<p dir="ltr"><span>Most of the sessions will be run by Finnish Olympic Medalist, Mira Jalosuo and NCAA&nbsp;</span></p>
<p dir="ltr"><span>coaches/players.</span></p>

<p dir="ltr"><span>Points of Emphasis:</span></p>
<p dir="ltr"><span>&nbsp;</span></p>
<p dir="ltr"><span>Skating:</span></p>
<ul>
<li dir="ltr">
<p dir="ltr" role="presentation"><span>Each practice we will spend 10-15 minutes to teach the mechanics of defensive skating.</span></p>
</li>
</ul>
<p dir="ltr"><span>&nbsp;</span></p>
<p dir="ltr"><span>Gap Control:</span></p>
<ul>
<li dir="ltr">
<p dir="ltr" role="presentation"><span>Much attention is placed on eliminating space since gap control is one of the most important and hardest aspects of playing defense.</span></p>
</li>
</ul>
<p dir="ltr"><span>&nbsp;</span></p>
<p dir="ltr"><span>Angling</span></p>
<ul>
<li dir="ltr">
<p dir="ltr" role="presentation"><span>As a defenseman you want to control what the forwards do with the puck. The key thing is to force the puck carrier to the wall and not let them take shots. The proper technique to do this is taught during this clinic</span></p>
</li>
</ul>
<p dir="ltr"><span>&nbsp;</span></p>
<p dir="ltr"><span>Stickhandling</span></p>
<ul>
<li dir="ltr">
<p dir="ltr" role="presentation"><span>Defensemen also have to be able to stickhandle in order to make good breakout passes, join offensive rushes, create space, etc. Stickhandling skills will be improved during this camp as well.</span></p>
</li>
</ul>
<p dir="ltr"><span>&nbsp;</span></p>
<p dir="ltr"><span>Offensive skills</span></p>
<ul>
<li dir="ltr">
<p dir="ltr" role="presentation"><span>Much attention is placed on offensive skills. A lot of defensemen have a great shot but once they run out of time and space they are not able to use their shot. Tips will be given how to create more time and space on the blue line and also when to drive to the net.</span></p>
</li>
</ul>
<p dir="ltr"><span>&nbsp;</span></p>
<p dir="ltr"><span>Game Situations</span></p>
<ul>
<li dir="ltr">
<p dir="ltr" role="presentation"><span>Players will be placed into game like situations and will be taught proper techniques to play each&nbsp;</span></p>
</li>
</ul>
<p dir="ltr"><span>situation (1vs1, 2vs1, 3vs1, 3vs2 etc.). Players will be forced to think and react at a game speed. Also&nbsp;</span></p>
<p dir="ltr"><span>much attention is placed to teach offensive and transitional skills.</span></p>
<p dir="ltr"><span>&nbsp;</span></p>
<p dir="ltr"><span>Shot Blocking</span></p>
<ul>
<li dir="ltr">
<p dir="ltr" role="presentation"><span>Proper technique to block spot is taught during this camp since every time you block a shot is a chance&nbsp;</span></p>
</li>
</ul>
<p dir="ltr"><span>to create offence and also it is important learn how to safely do it.</span></p>
<p dir="ltr"><span>&nbsp;</span></p>
<p dir="ltr"><span>Net Front Coverage</span></p>
<ul>
<li dir="ltr">
<p dir="ltr" role="presentation"><span>I teach defenseman to always block shots and stand between the player who is shooting the puck and the player who is screening the goalie. The reason why we do it differently than most training programs is pretty simple. First, defenseman should not be afraid to take bruises. Second, if for some reason the puck hits the goalie the rebound is never right in front of the goalie and the defenseman will have enough time to react. Third, when the defenseman blocks the shot we have a chance to create odd man rush since one of their players is outside the game.</span></p>
</li>
</ul>
<p dir="ltr"><span>&nbsp;</span></p>
<p dir="ltr"><span>Proper Contact Protocol</span></p>
<ul>
<li dir="ltr">
<p dir="ltr" role="presentation"><span>Huge part of defensive game is body contact. Defenseman has to be able to win corner battles, battles in front of the net etc. Also body contact skills make defensemen to play with more confidence. I am going to teach players proper checking technique how not to get penalties and how gain possession of the puck.</span></p>
</li>
</ul>

<p>Mira grew up playing boys youth hockey in Lieksa, Finland. In 9th grade, Mira was recruited to play in the Finnish women’s national league. During her four seasons playing for the Gophers, she won two National Championships (2012 and 2013). In that short period of time Mira went on to win a National Championship in 2014 and European Cup in 2015. She was also selected as the best defender of the European Cup tournament. 2015-2016 Mira played for the Minnesota WhiteCaps and got recruited to go play the playoffs in Sweden where she won the Swedish League championship. 2016-2017 season Mira played for Oulu Kärpät women’s team and won the Finnish League championship and was selected as the MVP of the playoffs. She also coached with the Stillwater Area Hockey Association, leading the U12A team to a 42-6-2 record in 2018-19.
