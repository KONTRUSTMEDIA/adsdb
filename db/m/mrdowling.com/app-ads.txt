				<h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">The Renaissance Spreads</h1>		<div class="entry-meta">
			<span class="byline">by <span class="author vcard" itemprop="author" itemtype="https://schema.org/Person" itemscope><a class="url fn n" href="https://mrdowling.com/author/admin" title="View all posts by Mike Dowling" rel="author" itemprop="url"><span class="author-name" itemprop="name">Mike Dowling</span></a></span></span> 		</div>
					</header>
			
		<div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
			<figure class="float-image">
  <img decoding="async" src="https://mrdowling.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/globe-theater.jpg" alt="Shakespeare's Globe Theatre in London"><figcaption class="image-caption">The Globe Theatre in London is a modern reproduction of the playhouse where William Shakespeare’s works were originally performed. The original Globe did not have stage lighting.</figcaption></figure>
<p>The rebirth of the Italian cities attracted visitors from all over Western Europe. Merchants and bankers hoped to make their fortunes in the Italian city-states. Artists and students sought knowledge and fame. When these travelers returned home, they brought Renaissance ideas with them. In time, the ideas of the Renaissance influenced people far from the Italian peninsula.</p>
<p>Italian Renaissance painters often focused on religious subjects, but the art of the Low Countries of northwest Europe was more concerned with portraits, landscapes, and scenes of everyday life. This was partly because most people of the Low Countries were Calvinists—a branch of Christianity that believes in building simple churches without ornamentation.</p>
<figure class="float-image">
  <img decoding="async" src="https://mrdowling.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/704Bruegel.jpg" alt="The Peasant Wedding by Pieter Bruegel the Elder"><figcaption class="image-caption"><em>The Peasant Wedding</em> (c. 1567) by Pieter Bruegel the Elder of Flanders. Bruegel’s art depicted peasants as they actually lived—working, dancing, and celebrating.</figcaption></figure>
<p>Pieter Bruegel the Elder (1525–1569) was from Flanders, a Dutch-speaking land that is now part of Belgium. Bruegel studied Italian art, but he developed a different style. He wanted to show people as they actually lived. Many of his paintings show peasants working, dancing, and eating.</p>
<p>England’s William Shakespeare (1564–1616) is the best-known writer of the Renaissance. His plays mixed humor with drama and revealed the strengths and weaknesses of human character. Audiences flocked to see his portrayals of Roman emperors, British monarchs, and Italian lovers.</p>
