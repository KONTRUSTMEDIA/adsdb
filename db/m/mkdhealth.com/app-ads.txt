		<h1 style="color: #2c3e50;">Mini Cottage Cheese Flatbreads</h1>
<p>Hey there, it&#8217;s Steve from , and in this video I&#8217;m going to show you my revision of the Viral Cottage Cheese Flatbread. When I released my video of the first time I tried making the Viral Cottage Cheese Flatbread, I got a lot of really snarky replies about how I didn&#8217;t follow the recipe. Well, I did follow a recipe.</p>
<p><img decoding="async" style="--smush-placeholder-width: 1024px; --smush-placeholder-aspect-ratio: 1024/1024;width: auto; height: 100%; display: block; margin-bottom: 15px;" data-src="https://mkdhealth.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/image-812.png" alt="Mini Cottage Cheese Flatbreads" src="data:image/svg+xml;base64,PHN2ZyB3aWR0aD0iMSIgaGVpZ2h0PSIxIiB4bWxucz0iaHR0cDovL3d3dy53My5vcmcvMjAwMC9zdmciPjwvc3ZnPg==" class="lazyload" /></p>
<h2 style="color: #34495e; border-bottom: 2px solid #8e44ad; padding-bottom: 5px;">Why You’ll Love This</h2>
<p>The cool thing about failure, though, is that oftentimes it is the inspiration for other people.</p>
<p>My goal with creating this new process was, what can I do to create consistent results with the easiest recipe possible, and be something that I could make in bulk?</p>
<p>And what I came up with are these little mini flatbreads. They&#8217;re nice and pliable, you can see it&#8217;s not cracking.</p>
<p>If I wanted, I could fill this up with some pulled pork or something.</p>
<p>Or I could take two of them and put a sausage patty in there with cheese.</p>
<p>If you&#8217;ve tried the viral cottage cheese flatbread and either it didn&#8217;t work for you or you just felt it was a little too much work for the payoff, maybe give this a try.</p>
<p><img decoding="async" style="--smush-placeholder-width: 1024px; --smush-placeholder-aspect-ratio: 1024/1024;width: auto; height: 100%; display: block; margin-bottom: 15px;" data-src="https://mkdhealth.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/image-810.png" alt="Ingredients for Mini Cottage Cheese Flatbreads" src="data:image/svg+xml;base64,PHN2ZyB3aWR0aD0iMSIgaGVpZ2h0PSIxIiB4bWxucz0iaHR0cDovL3d3dy53My5vcmcvMjAwMC9zdmciPjwvc3ZnPg==" class="lazyload" /></p>
<h2 style="color: #34495e; border-bottom: 2px solid #8e44ad; padding-bottom: 5px;">Ingredients</h2>
<ul style="list-style-type: disc; margin-left: 20px;">
<li>one cup of full-fat cottage cheese or 285 grams</li>
<li>two large eggs</li>
<li>everything bagel seasoning</li>
<li>one half teaspoon seasoning</li>
<li>avocado oil</li>
</ul>
<p><img decoding="async" style="--smush-placeholder-width: 1024px; --smush-placeholder-aspect-ratio: 1024/1024;width: auto; height: 100%; display: block; margin-bottom: 15px;" data-src="https://mkdhealth.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/image-811.png" alt="Instructions for Mini Cottage Cheese Flatbreads" src="data:image/svg+xml;base64,PHN2ZyB3aWR0aD0iMSIgaGVpZ2h0PSIxIiB4bWxucz0iaHR0cDovL3d3dy53My5vcmcvMjAwMC9zdmciPjwvc3ZnPg==" class="lazyload" /></p>
<h2 style="color: #34495e; border-bottom: 2px solid #8e44ad; padding-bottom: 5px;">Instructions</h2>
<ol style="margin-left: 20px;">
<li>We&#8217;ll start with one cup of full-fat cottage cheese.</li>
<li>two large eggs.</li>
<li>And then we&#8217;re going to process this for about 10 to 20 seconds or until it&#8217;s mostly smooth.</li>
<li>Then using a spatula, I&#8217;m going to scrape down the edges and give this probably another five seconds.</li>
<li>After that, I&#8217;m going to add my seasoning.</li>
<li>Give this another three or four pulses, and our batter is done.</li>
<li>I&#8217;m using a silicone mold for buns or mini cakes.</li>
<li>And I&#8217;m going to spray this with some avocado oil.</li>
<li>So once I spray each one of the compartments, I&#8217;m going to have to give it a little rub down with my fingertip here.</li>
<li>And then I&#8217;m going to divide this batter evenly among all six compartments.</li>
<li>Then I&#8217;m going to tap this on the counter a few times, just to get rid of any air bubbles, make sure everything is settled.</li>
<li>And then this will go into my oven, preheated to 350 degrees or 175 degrees Celsius for 25 minutes.</li>
<li>At the halfway point, 12, 13 minutes in, I&#8217;m going to rotate this just to account for any hot or cold spots that might be in my oven and make sure that they cook hopefully pretty evenly.</li>
<li>After the full 25 minutes has elapsed, I&#8217;m going to take this out of the oven and you can see that they&#8217;ve kind of puffed up a little bit, almost like a souffle, but we&#8217;re going to let this rest for five minutes and these will shrink down.</li>
<li>You can see how these have shrunk down nicely and they&#8217;ve cooled off enough that I can flip them over and cook the other side just briefly.</li>
<li>So after I flip all six of them, I&#8217;ll pop this back into the oven for an additional five minutes.</li>
</ol>
<p><img decoding="async" style="--smush-placeholder-width: 1024px; --smush-placeholder-aspect-ratio: 1024/1024;width: auto; height: 100%; display: block; margin-bottom: 15px;" data-src="https://mkdhealth.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/image-809.png" alt="Cooking tips for Mini Cottage Cheese Flatbreads" src="data:image/svg+xml;base64,PHN2ZyB3aWR0aD0iMSIgaGVpZ2h0PSIxIiB4bWxucz0iaHR0cDovL3d3dy53My5vcmcvMjAwMC9zdmciPjwvc3ZnPg==" class="lazyload" /></p>
<h2 style="color: #34495e; border-bottom: 2px solid #8e44ad; padding-bottom: 5px;">Cooking Tips</h2>
<p>Season however you want. However, I do recommend limiting yourself to one half teaspoon, as a little goes a long way.</p>
<p>At the halfway point, 12, 13 minutes in, I&#8217;m going to rotate this just to account for any hot or cold spots that might be in my oven and make sure that they cook hopefully pretty evenly.</p>
<p>We&#8217;re going to let this rest for five minutes and these will shrink down.</p>
<p>The underside is still a little bit moist. So after I flip all six of them, I&#8217;ll pop this back into the oven for an additional five minutes.</p>
<p>If you&#8217;re wondering, couldn&#8217;t you just do this in a waffle iron and make a chaffle? Maybe, but the great thing about this process and recipe is that you can do them in bulk.</p>
<p>If I want to do, say, 12 at a time, not a problem, just two cookie sheets.</p>
<p>If you can find any sort of cooking tray or baking sheet that has got an internal dimension of 13 and a half by nine inches, these fit in there perfectly.</p>
<p>You could do four of these, two per shelf in your oven and make 24 of these buns or whatever you want to call them at once.</p>
<p>Then if you want, just stack them up with a little pieces of parchment paper or wax paper in between them, throw them in a Ziploc bag, freeze them, and you&#8217;ve got breakfast sandwich buns on demand.</p>
<p>As for reheating, you can do this in a microwave or just pop a couple in the toaster.</p>
<h2 style="color: #34495e; border-bottom: 2px solid #8e44ad; padding-bottom: 5px;">Serving Suggestions</h2>
<p>If I wanted, I could fill this up with some pulled pork or something.</p>
<p>Or I could take two of them and put a sausage patty in there with cheese.</p>
<p>Then top it with some sausage, a little cheese, and throw a lid on it. Either side is fine. There&#8217;s your breakfast sandwich.</p>
<p>It&#8217;s like a sausage McMuffin, just without the muffin.</p>
<p>So if you&#8217;ve tried the viral cottage cheese flatbread and either it didn&#8217;t work for you or you just felt it was a little too much work for the payoff, maybe give this a try.</p>
<p>But that is it for this video. As always, Thanks for watching.</p>
