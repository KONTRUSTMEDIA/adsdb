<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-4049082711137806804' itemprop='articleBody'>
<blockquote><strong><em><span style="font-size:180%;"> Where there&#8217;s a will &#8230; there&#8217;s relatives.</span></em></strong></blockquote>A <b><i>paraprosdokian</i></b> is wordplay where the latter part of a statement forces you to reinterpret the first part. The phrase intentionally leads you in one direction&#8212;misleads you into thinking one way&#8212;and then abruptly changes direction, ending with the unexpected twist. The origin of the word is Greek, meaning <i>"against expectation</i>."<br />
<br />
Most of the statements in these weekly blog posts are actual paraprosdokians; you might possibly dispute a few. But since they&#8217;re still fun plays on words, I&#8217;m sharing them anyway.<br />
<br />
Because paraprosdokians come in two parts, I present each part as a separate quote, each revealed by solving its own set of word Sudoku puzzles.<br />
