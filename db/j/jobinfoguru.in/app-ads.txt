											<h1>10 Major Government Job Deadlines Closing Today: Apply Now for TNPSC, OFB, AIIMS &#038; More</h1>
<p><strong>Source:</strong> <a href="https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/education/news/these-10-government-jobs-are-expiring-today-direct-link-to-apply-for-tnpsc-ofb-aiims-nhm-ap-high-court-and-others-here/articleshow/122778358.cms" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Times of India</a></p>
<h2>Introduction: A Critical Juncture for Government Job Seekers in India</h2>
<p>Today marks a significant turning point for aspirants across India seeking employment in the public sector. A total of 180+ government job vacancies are set to close by the end of the day, spanning across some of the most sought-after institutions including the Tamil Nadu Public Service Commission (TNPSC), Ordnance Factory Board (OFB), All India Institute of Medical Sciences (AIIMS), National Health Mission (NHM), and the Andhra Pradesh High Court.</p>
<p>The urgency comes not only from the volume and prestige of these openings but also from the diversity of roles on offer — encompassing administrative, technical, legal, and healthcare domains. For individuals navigating the complexity of government exam timelines and application procedures, today represents a vital window of opportunity.</p>
<p>Beyond recruitment, these roles carry weighty implications for career stability, social status, and long-term growth — all of which are integral to the Indian employment landscape. In this article, we offer a timely synthesis of available job avenues, updated insights from verified sources, and a roadmap for immediate action.</p>
<h2>Key Highlights: Summary of Jobs Closing Today</h2>
<h3>Institutions &#038; Roles</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>TNPSC (Tamil Nadu Public Service Commission):</strong> Multiple administrative posts under Group-IV services</li>
<li><strong>OFB (Ordnance Factory Board):</strong> Apprenticeship positions across technical trades</li>
<li><strong>AIIMS (All India Institute of Medical Sciences):</strong> Nurse, medical attendant, and teaching faculty positions</li>
<li><strong>NHM (National Health Mission):</strong> Public health professionals, community health officers</li>
<li><strong>AP High Court:</strong> Clerical staff and assistants under various bilateral recruitment drives</li>
</ul>
<h3>Application Window</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Deadline:</strong> Most applications will close by midnight today (IST)</li>
<li>Applications must be submitted through respective institutional portals</li>
<li>Late submissions will not be accepted under standard recruitment protocols</li>
</ul>
<h3>Eligibility Summary</h3>
<ul>
<li>Minimum educational qualifications vary by role: 10th pass to graduate-level</li>
<li>Age limits typically range between 18 and 40 years (as per position)</li>
<li>Category-based age relaxations are standard as per government norms</li>
</ul>
<p>For direct links, official documents, and detailed eligibility, refer to <a href="https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/education/news/these-10-government-jobs-are-expiring-today-direct-link-to-apply-for-tnpsc-ofb-aiims-nhm-ap-high-court-and-others-here/articleshow/122778358.cms" target="_blank" rel="noopener">this overview by the Times of India</a>.</p>
<h2>Cross-checking with Other Official Data and Available Opportunities</h2>
<p>To provide further granularity, we reviewed connected job boards and official departmental museums, including the UPSC calendar, sarkari result archives, and high court recruitment cells. Here is an evidence-based breakdown from these sources:</p>
<h3>Additional Reliable Source Checks</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>National Career Service Portal (Ministry of Labour &#038; Employment):</strong> Confirms parallel listings for NHM and OFB-related apprenticeships</li>
<li><strong>AIIMS Official Careers Page:</strong> Currently holding walk-in interviews and electronic applications for paramedical staff till end of May</li>
<li><strong>TNPSC.gov.in:</strong> Confirms final deadline on the Group-IV application process aligns with today’s date</li>
</ul>
<p>Furthermore, the AP High Court has clarified that no extension in deadlines will be granted, even under exceptional situations, a trend mirrored by TNPSC and AIIMS administrations.</p>
<p>In closely following updates from verified employment portals such as <a href="https://www.ncs.gov.in" target="_blank" rel="noopener">ncs.gov.in</a> and <a href="https://sarkariresult.com" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Sarkari Result</a>, it is evident that shortlisting and call letter dispatch phases will begin promptly after today’s deadline. Thus, completion of forms and document uploads must be treated as time-sensitive actions.</p>
<h2>India-Specific Employment Trends and Context</h2>
<p>The significance of these job announcements must be understood within the broader Indian employment ecosystem. According to the Centre for Monitoring Indian Economy (CMIE), India’s unemployment rate stood at 7.8% in May 2024. Public sector jobs remain highly coveted due to their security, pension benefits, and structured promotion paths.</p>
<p>The following trends shape applicant sentiment:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Urban vs. Rural Divide:</strong> Government opportunities are especially critical in Tier-2 and Tier-3 regions where private sector hiring remains sluggish.</li>
<li><strong>Digital Penetration in Application Process:</strong> With digital-first portals now standard, the digital divide affects under-connected populations despite rising interest.</li>
<li><strong>Policy Push from Central Government:</strong> With recent announcements under “Rozgar Mela,” direct recruitment to central posts has become more aggressive in 2024.</li>
</ul>
<p>It is within this context that urgent job application closures gain prominence, not only as employment options but as part of the government&#8217;s economic stimulus architecture.</p>
<h2>Immediate Steps: What Should Applicants Do Now?</h2>
<p>Applicants still considering whether to act should note that inactivity may result in complete disqualification, as no extension notifications have been issued. Use the checklist below:</p>
