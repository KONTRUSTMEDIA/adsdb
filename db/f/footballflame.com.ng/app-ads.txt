                <h1>FIFA-approved app allows players to report match-fixing</h1>                <div class="posted-on"><i class="mdi-clock-time-three-outline"></i><time class="entry-date published updated" datetime="2020-09-10T14:14:00+01:00">September 10, 2020</time></div>            </header>

<article id="post-5721" class="vn-article-content post-5721 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-news" >

    <div class="entry-content">

        <div style="text-align: justify;">
<div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEi0y__tiZfLELS4xkwCPx41KXWIUIfD1QVOVrbhrJ3y_J6HXV0VSTDrCt5F6LIQzqNShN-J9uXrofC6GSDs59YWd8fw6J1Xja37pYIVI3x7VCHxEgY4BiOm9gloYtvHmc7m9Ucv9P4acsMu/s1600/gettyimages-1208248837-612x612.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img bv-data-src="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEi0y__tiZfLELS4xkwCPx41KXWIUIfD1QVOVrbhrJ3y_J6HXV0VSTDrCt5F6LIQzqNShN-J9uXrofC6GSDs59YWd8fw6J1Xja37pYIVI3x7VCHxEgY4BiOm9gloYtvHmc7m9Ucv9P4acsMu/s1600/gettyimages-1208248837-612x612.jpg"  class="bv-tag-attr-replace bv-lazyload-tag-img"  decoding="async" border="0" data-original-height="407" data-original-width="612" src="data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%200%200'%3E%3C/svg%3E" /></a></div>
<p><i>POLAND &#8211; 2020/03/23: In this photo illustration a FIFA logo seen displayed on a smartphone.</i></p>
<p>World governing body FIFA has approved an app designed to help professional players report match-fixing approaches.</p></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
</div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
The Red Button app, which is owned by world players&#8217; union FIFPRO, will be distributed to players globally via national unions such as England&#8217;s Professional Footballers&#8217; Association (PFA).</div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
</div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
It complements existing reporting tools set up by FIFA, and information received via the app will be investigated by the world governing body&#8217;s integrity department.</div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
</div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
The Europol agency reported in August that football is the sport which is most heavily targeted by criminals involved in fixing.</div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
</div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
FIFPRO&#8217;s legal director Roy Vermeer said: &#8220;We are confident this will strengthen the hand of football and public authorities in the fight against match fixing.</div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
</div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
&#8220;With players facing disciplinary action for failing to report a match-fixing approach, there must be a way for them to do this without fear that they are putting themselves, their families and their careers in danger.</div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
</div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
&#8220;The Red Button app provides this facility and will help players manage this considerable risk that, through no fault of their own, might confront them at any time.&#8221;&nbsp;</div>
