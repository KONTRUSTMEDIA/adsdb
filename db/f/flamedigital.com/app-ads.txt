				<h1 class="entry-title">						<a href="https://flamedigital.com/learn-about-veneers/" title="Permalink to Learn About Veneers" rel="bookmark">Learn About Veneers</a></h1>
			</header><!-- .entry-header -->

			<div class="entry-content"><p>Dental veneers, thin layers of porcelain or plastic material placed over the surface of teeth to to make small improvements to the appearance of teeth.</p>
<p>Dental veneers are custom-made to fit each patient&#8217;s unique mouth structure, bite and teeth alignment.</p>
<p>Although veneers are a cosmetic procedure, they can also improve the function of your mouth. For example, they can be used to correct minor imperfections in tooth color, shape and alignment. Fill large holes or gaps in the teeth, creating a smooth-looking finish.</p>
<h3><strong>Benefits of dental veneers</strong></h3>
<ol>
<li>Improve the structurally faulty teeth, for example by correcting part or all missing teeth or one crooked tooth. It is perfect for patients who are suffering from TMJ syndrome (jaw pain due to jaw joint dysfunction).</li>
<li>Cosmetic Improvement, such as tooth discoloration, minimal tooth size, alignment issues and other cosmetic effects can be improved with dental veneers.</li>
<li>Dental veneer procedures also help improve oral hygiene by covering teeth, thus reducing plaque and bacteria growth on them.. A veneer may help you avoid the need for bridges by restoring full-fledge, healthy teeth.</li>
<li>Cover small chips or cracks on the teeth. Dental veneers are made of a hard, high-strength plastic material that doesn&#8217;t chip or flake easily and has excellent strength and bonding qualities.</li>
<li>Improve facial contour by matching tooth placement to facial features and proportions.</li>
</ol>
<h3><strong>Who are dental veneers for?</strong></h3>
<p>Dental veneers are ideal for anybody who wants to improve their smile, retain structural integrity and not undergo invasive dental treatment. This cosmetic procedure is performed by a Tacoma dentist. Common candidates for dental veneers include those with:</p>
<ul>
<li> A single, missing tooth, including the tooth closest to the cheekbone</li>
<li> Crooked teeth, or teeth with gaps or an excessive number of teeth Crowded or spaced teeth</li>
<li> Teeth with chips or cracks or missing teeth caused by accidents or oral diseases such as periodontal disease, dental decay and tooth abscesses</li>
<li> People who have undergone a lot of dental work, like braces and root canals, which can be corrected by dental veneers</li>
<li> Individuals with discoloration or staining on their teeth(an orange- or yellowish-brown stain on teeth created by a staining agent)</li>
<li>Teeth that are severely chipped or cracked but are not missing enough material to warrant crowns</li>
</ul>
<h3><strong> How long does a dental veneer procedure take?</strong></h3>
<p><img decoding="async" class="alignright size-full wp-image-132" src="http://www.flamedigital.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/09/dental-veneers-model.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="166" srcset="https://flamedigital.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/09/dental-veneers-model.jpg 300w, https://flamedigital.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/09/dental-veneers-model-250x138.jpg 250w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" />Depending on the extent of the treatment, a <a href="https://www.newmouth.com/dentistry/cosmetic/veneers/">veneer procedure</a> can take between one to three visits. On average, most treatments require two visits. When you&#8217;re scheduling your appointment, consider allowing at least a month between appointments to allow enough time for healing and settling in of the new tooth structure.</p>
<p><strong>Conclusion: </strong></p>
<p>Dental veneers are very thin, highly durable and esthetically pleasing. They look like natural teeth. Teeth that have been treated with veneers are in this way improved in appearance, structure and health.</p>
<p>Although dental veneers cannot be used to treat all cases of tooth problems, they can be used to improve the appearance of bad teeth. It provides a simple solution to a complex problem of improving the natural appearance of your smile without undergoing invasive dental treatment or extensive cosmetic dentistry procedures.</p>

<p><b>Introduction with sedation dentistry</b></p>
<p>If before doing any dental procedure if the dentist has to use a drug like general anesthesia etc. to make the patient completely unconscious then it is known as sedation dentistry. Though various s other methods are also used by dentists to relax their patients but they do not help in relaxing them completely.</p>
<p><b>For whom Sedation Dentistry is suitable</b></p>
<p>Sedation dentistry is considered suitable for certain patients for different reasons like:</p>
<ul>
<li>Bad experience with previous work on teeth</li>
<li>Dental procedure phobia</li>
<li>Soreness in the small mouth during dental procedures</li>
<li>Sensitivity in certain oral nerves</li>
<li>Anxiety disorder in general</li>
<li>Problem with local anesthesia</li>
</ul>
<p><img decoding="async" class="alignright size-full wp-image-93" src="/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/smilingpatient.jpg" alt="" width="300" />You can ask your dentist for sedation dentistry if you have any of the problems discussed above. <p><b>Types of sedation dentistry</b></p>
<p>Generally, three types of sedation dentistry are used, including:</p>
<ul>
<li>The use of nitrous oxide gas is done to relax the patients during dental procedures. You can go back to your home yourself after the procedure as its effect will be over after some time.</li>
<li>The use of oral sedatives like diazepam etc. is done to relax patients during procedures. You should take these sedatives nearly an hour before the procedure. It will keep you a bit less anxious but awake during the procedure. Until the effect of local sedative is over you may feel a bit sleepy.</li>
<li>The use of IV or intravenous sedatives is done to keep the patients unconscious at different stages of the procedure. Due to the effect of this anesthesia, the patient will not be aware of his surroundings and sleep deeply until its effect is over.</li>
</ul>
<p>Though sedation dentistry is considered as the best option by many patients still it should be avoided as much as possible as it can cause several side effects. However, if you prefer it then you should tell your dentist about your allergies and sensitivities before using any kind of sedation dentistry.</p>
<p>If sedation dentistry is not suitable for you then you can opt for using local anesthesia to numb your mouth so that you may not experience any pain during a dental procedure.</p>
<p><b>Working of sedation dentistry</b></p>
<p>The type of sedation used by the dentist can change its working process. A sedative drug will be prescribed by your dentist with an instruction to use it if oral sedative is recommended by him. You can remain relaxed and reduce your anxiety during the dental procedure if you follow the instructions of your dentist. You will start feeling relaxed and drowsy as the drug will start working on you.</p>
<p>If you are recommended for the use of nitrous oxide then no prior preparation is required as your dentist over at <a href="https://www.puresmiles.com/westerville/">Pure Smiles &#8211; Westerville</a> can give it to the patient any time when required, before, during, or after the procedure.</p>
<p>But, for intravenous or IV sedation, you will have to be prepared in advance. You may not be allowed to drink or eat anything including your regular medications a few hours before the dental procedure with IV sedation.</p>
<p><b>The benefits of Sedation Dentistry</b></p>
<p><b>Reduce your fear:</b> The use of any of the methods of sedation dentistry will reduce your fear for the dental chair as well as of the needles used during a dental procedure.</p>
<p><b>Reduce the risk for general health</b>: If you are ignoring dental procedures due to your fear of pain caused by your Baton Rouge LA migraines then it can increase the risk of worsening your dental problems. In such situations, sedation dentistry can be the best solution for you. Some of your dental problems like broken or loose teeth, dental caries, etc. can increase the risk for your overall health if ignored for a long time.</p>
<p>So, sedation dentistry can be a good method of dentistry for those who are worried about the dental procedures to be done on them. They can feel relaxed and comfortable in the dental chair after using this method.</p>

<p>Deep cleaning is also known as scaling and root planing. It includes cleaning of the outer surface of the roots and below the gum line. Deep cleaning is needed when the tartar has infected your gums.</p>
<p><strong>Flossing</strong></p>
<p>During dental floss, a thin chord of filament is used to remove food and plaque stuck between the teeth. These food particles get stuck in areas that are hard to reach with a toothbrush.</p>
<p><strong>Treating with fluoride</strong></p>
<p>Fluoride treatment is the last step in the cleaning process. It protects your teeth and fights against cavities for several months after the dental cleaning. Fluoride forms a foamy gel in your mouth which strengthens the enamel of your teeth by forming a hard layer on it.</p>
<p><strong>Antibiotic prophylaxis</strong></p>
<p>Antibiotic prophylaxis is a premedication for a dental cleaning. Taking antibiotics before teeth cleaning prevents your teeth and gums from infection due to germs and bacteria.</p>
<p><strong>Nutrition counseling</strong></p>
<p>Your dentist advises the diet and nutrition for healthy teeth and gums. He examines your current nutrition habits and ensures to keep your oral system strong and healthy.</p>
<p>He also educates you on best practices, brushing, flossing and other dental care tips for your oral hygiene.</p>
<p>The above guide provides you a brief overview of the professional dental cleanings and their benefits. It also explains the various processes and their purpose in dental cleaning. You can get these services and benefits when you visit a professional dentist regularly.</p>

<p>To most of us a visit to the dentist while growing up was always a horrendous encounter. The nervousness and trauma was almost unbearable, this is due to the pain one was expecting to face. But with the new and ever advancing technology new ways of countering this painful procedure are being introduced. This is all in a bid to change the notion that dentistry has carried that all procedure are pain staking.</p>
<p><u>Sleep dentistry </u></p>
<p>This is one of the safer methods that involves sedation. In <a href="http://www.webmd.com/oral-health/sedation-dentistry-can-you-really-relax-in-the-dentists-chair">sleep dentistry</a> a sedationist administers the drug intravenously to allow the patient to be fully relaxed during the procedure. <p>An injection technique was finally developed to deal with the pain involved slow delivery system which is able to administer a painless injection . It has been welcomed immensely since most people see it as a way if making a visit to the dentist a rather pleasant experience.</p>
<p><u>Oral sedation </u></p>
<p>Most of the sedation methods may involve a shoot or some injection but it’s not the case with oral sedation . It offers complete relaxation through a pill.</p>
<p>With oral sedation one is able to experience complete relaxation and a relief from dental anxiety, this is more so because it enables one to see the entire procedure taking place . Unlike other methods one is able to witness the procedure since they are still conscious.</p>
<p><u>Nitrous oxide </u></p>
<p>Also known as &#8220;laughing gas &#8221; it is known to give a calming effect right before a procedure is done. It helps a lot in numbing the nerves and even helps in relieving the dental anxiety that one may be having.</p>
<p>Another commonly used anesthesia is Novocain, it is one of the most commonly used especially in dental procedures. It is effective and very much available but it’s down side is that it is not fit for use on everyone. Some may react negatively towards it .</p>
<p><u>Conclusion</u></p>
<p>It is clear that over the years more and more ways are coming up as to how the trauma associated with a visit to the Roseville dentist can be avoided. Technology has brought in new ways which are completely pain free and don&#8217;t involve long term sedation with minimal side effects.</p>
<p>Methods like computer generated anesthesia and electronic anesthesia have proven that the methods don&#8217;t need to involve needles. They simplify the whole task that initially seemed like a mountain to climb to a super simple procedure. Therefore, these methods can definitely be counted upon.</p>

			<div class="entry-content"><p><center><iframe loading="lazy" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/cGxth9OJl0A" width="560" height="315" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen"></iframe></center>Cosmetic dentists who have the skills and knowledge of dental surgical procedures perform various operations that are meant to improve one’s teeth. Most dental problems affect the appearance of the teeth. These problems include: loss of teeth, teeth cavities, gum diseases, tooth decay and discoloration of teeth. Cosmetic dentists’ experts provide services to affected patients basing on the infection that the teeth of the patient has. Some of these services are as discussed below:</p>
<p><strong>Root canals</strong></p>
<p>This method of treatment involves the removal of pulp canal. Pulp is a thread like and small tissue that is usually located around the tooth center. The pulp contains lymph tissue, nerves and blood vessels. Once the damaged pulp is removed, the space that remains is cleaned, shaped and filled. In case the decay extends to the lower level of the pulp, tooth aching is experienced. This also exposes the tooth infection risks. Thus, the root canal dental procedure involves the sealing of the canal to prevent the patient from infections as well as in reducing pain. A pus may build up at the tip of the root destroying surrounding bone which in most cases causes severe pain if the pulp that is diseased, dead or damaged is left untreated. Root canal treatment can be used to treat teeth injuries, cracked teeth as well as deep cavities.</p>
<p><strong>Dental crowns</strong></p>
<p><a title="dental crowns" href="http://www.mouthhealthy.org/en/az-topics/c/Crowns">Dental crowns</a> are the most common methods of teeth treatment procedures. They are commonly referred to ascaps since they sit on the upper part of the tooth entirely. They improve the general appearance of the tooth and also improve on the strength. They are made from different materials such as ceramic, metals, stainless steel as well as resin. They cover discolored teeth.</p>
<p><strong>Dental implants</strong></p>
<p>They have a more natural look compared to the dentures. They are usually installed in the jaw bone and gum to act as normal tooth root. The deep foundation provides room for replacement teeth that that can be permanently fixed or temporal. They restore comfort, improved appearance of the teeth, confidence when communicating as well as easy chewing of food. The top La Crescenta dentist insert these implants basing on the teeth design. They are considered to be very safe since the material that they are made of, titanium, has no side effects to the body.</p>
<p><strong>Dentures</strong></p>
<p>These dental procedures are suitable to patients lacking multiple teeth. They are also suitable to the elderly who have lost their teeth naturally or those people who have been involved in accidents leading to loss of some teeth. They are more affordable procedures as compared to the dental implants since they are cheap. They restore the smile and appearance of the teeth lost by multiple loss of teeth. These procedures may generate some pain and therefore use of general anesthesia is recommended.</p>

				<h1 class="entry-title">						<a href="https://flamedigital.com/how-a-filling-works/" title="Permalink to How A Filling Works" rel="bookmark">How A Filling Works</a></h1>
			</header><!-- .entry-header -->

			<div class="entry-content"><p><center><iframe loading="lazy" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/TyyslHitgcM" width="640" height="480" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen"></iframe></center>Fillings are used in the treatment of cavities. Cavities are generally treated to remove the portion of the tooth that has decayed and to reconstruct any structure of the tooth that is missing with a filing element.</p>
<p><strong>Working of the filling.</strong></p>
<p>The dentists in most cases begins with an injection to relieve any pain in the tooth by use of anesthetic. Xylocaine is mostly used as the anesthetic. The tooth is then removed from the mouth. The decay present in the tooth is ejected and the tooth prepared for filling. The tooth is prepared accordingly taking into considerations which material is used. The sensitivity of the tooth is reduced by a liner. Examples of liners include: gluma and dycal. Dycal contains calcium hydroxide and is useful in deep cavities. Bases are mixed with liner in deep fillings. Such bases include: zinc phosphate. Bases help in protecting the teeth from varying temperature conditions in the mouth. The most common materials for filling the tooth are porcelain, silver, resin and gold. The dentist in Citrus Heights put these materials over the base or liner to complete the process of reconstructing the tooth.</p>
<p><strong>Recovery.</strong></p>
<p>A tooth that has been filled takes about two days to lose its sensitivity. However, the deeper the filling, the more time required for the tooth to lose its sensitivity. A filling completely recovers after approximately two weeks. In case, the filling builds to higher levels, a second visit to the dentist is required to bring back the filling to a more comfortable level. Sensitivity should be over within two weeks otherwise there are some voids beneath the filling. If the discomfort also persist, it indicates that the tooth has infected pulp and root canal therapy process is needed to remove it with immediate effect.</p>
<p><strong>Types of fillings</strong>.</p>
<p>There are many materials that can be used for <a title="teeth filling" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dental_restoration">teeth filling</a>. Some of two common filing materials include:</p>
<p><strong>Amalgam fillings</strong>: they are made of metals such as tin, zinc, copper and silver. They are also known as mercury of silver fillings. Dental amalgam is safe since the mercury present is locked inside during the process of hardening of the filling hence not harmful. They are recommended on the back teeth since they are strong and hence able to resist the forces involved in chewing of food.</p>
<p><strong>Composite fillings</strong>: these are more improved than the amalgam fillings. They are also known as white filings and are composed of tiny pieces of silica. These pieces are surrounded by a plastic resin.</p>
<p><strong>Why are filings necessary?</strong></p>
<p>Different habits of feeding and eating of junky foods without brushing your teeth results to accumulation of bacteria in your teeth. These bacteria feed on the food that accumulates on the teeth and produce a strong acid that damages the teeth. This in turn leads to development of a sticky bacteria layer called plaque. Holes develop in teeth causing cavities, dental caries and tooth decay. This can be prevented by use of fillings to recover the appearance of the teeth.</p>
