Before you grab a &#8220;quick fix&#8221; fiber supplement, focus on food first, says Molly Morgan, RD, CDN, CSSD, author of Drink Your Way to <a href="https://www.healthline.com/health/digestive-health/things-your-gut-wants-you-to-know" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Gut Health</a>. Here, are our top food picks to help you <a href="https://thecompleteherbalguide.com/entries/constipation/natural-and-herbal-treatments-used-to-heal-constipation-naturally/">poop</a>.</p>
<h2><img data-recalc-dims="1" decoding="async" class="size-full wp-image-53248 alignnone" src="https://i0.wp.com/thecompleteherbalguide.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/rasberry-tea.jpg?resize=750%2C500&#038;ssl=1" alt="rasberry tea" width="750" height="500" srcset="https://i0.wp.com/thecompleteherbalguide.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/rasberry-tea.jpg?w=750&amp;ssl=1 750w, https://i0.wp.com/thecompleteherbalguide.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/rasberry-tea.jpg?resize=300%2C200&amp;ssl=1 300w, https://i0.wp.com/thecompleteherbalguide.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/rasberry-tea.jpg?resize=100%2C67&amp;ssl=1 100w" sizes="(max-width: 750px) 100vw, 750px" /></h2>

<h2><span class="ez-toc-section" id="1_Raspberries"></span>1. Raspberries<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>
<p>Raspberries are fiber rock stars, with 8 g per cup—that&#8217;s double the fiber of <a href="https://thecompleteherbalguide.com/entries/underweight/use-10-herbs-efficient-healthy-weight-gain/">strawberries</a>! &#8220;Fiber increases the bulk of your stool to help food move smoothly through the digestive system; plus, it feeds good bacteria in the gut for optimal digestion,&#8221; says Erica Sonnenburg, Ph.D., a senior research scientist in the department of microbiology and immunology at Stanford University School of Medicine and co-author of The Good <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/ency/article/003131.htm">Gut</a>. Top your morning oatmeal or yogurt with raspberries or pop &#8217;em plain as a snack.</p>
<h2><img data-recalc-dims="1" decoding="async" class="size-full wp-image-54030 alignnone" src="https://i0.wp.com/thecompleteherbalguide.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/orange.jpg?resize=750%2C500&#038;ssl=1" alt="orange" width="750" height="500" srcset="https://i0.wp.com/thecompleteherbalguide.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/orange.jpg?w=750&amp;ssl=1 750w, https://i0.wp.com/thecompleteherbalguide.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/orange.jpg?resize=300%2C200&amp;ssl=1 300w, https://i0.wp.com/thecompleteherbalguide.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/orange.jpg?resize=100%2C67&amp;ssl=1 100w" sizes="(max-width: 750px) 100vw, 750px" /></h2>
<h2><span class="ez-toc-section" id="2_Oranges"></span>2. Oranges<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>
<p>The citrus powerhouse is a triple threat: Oranges have lots of stool-softening vitamin C, fiber to increase bulk in your <a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC4414762/">stool</a>, and naringenin, a flavonoid that researchers found can work as a laxative. Pack an orange as a portable <a href="https://thecompleteherbalguide.com/entries/nutrition/4-healthy-alternatives-to-soda/">snack</a> or add orange segments to your salad.</p>
<h2><img data-recalc-dims="1" loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="size-full wp-image-47902 alignnone" src="https://i0.wp.com/thecompleteherbalguide.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/02/water-3.jpg?resize=750%2C500&#038;ssl=1" alt="water" width="750" height="500" srcset="https://i0.wp.com/thecompleteherbalguide.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/02/water-3.jpg?w=750&amp;ssl=1 750w, https://i0.wp.com/thecompleteherbalguide.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/02/water-3.jpg?resize=300%2C200&amp;ssl=1 300w, https://i0.wp.com/thecompleteherbalguide.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/02/water-3.jpg?resize=100%2C67&amp;ssl=1 100w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 750px) 100vw, 750px" /></h2>
<h2><span class="ez-toc-section" id="3_Water"></span>3. Water<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>
<p>&#8220;Staying hydrated is essential to helping things move,&#8221; says Morgan. Without ample H2O, <a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23518359">stool</a> can&#8217;t soften and move smoothly through the digestive tract. No wonder dehydration is a common cause of constipation. Drink up: Sip straight H2O, and add lemon or cucumber slices for extra flavor</p>
<h2><span class="ez-toc-section" id="4_Kefir"></span>4. Kefir<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>
<p>The fermented dairy drink is packed with probiotics, &#8220;good&#8221; bacteria vital to <a href="https://thecompleteherbalguide.com/entries/vegetarian-vegan/the-digestive-perks-of-a-vegetarian/">gut health</a>. And kefir has 10 times more strains of bacteria than yogurt does, says Sonnenburg: &#8220;The greater diversity improves the chance that some of these microbes will be beneficial to your particular <a href="https://thecompleteherbalguide.com/entries/everyday-solutions/three-reasons-visit-local-gastroenterologist-identify-best-ones/">gut microbiota</a>,&#8221; she adds. What&#8217;s more, British researchers found that probiotics can ease <a href="https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/constipation/symptoms-causes/syc-20354253" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">constipation</a>, soften stools, and even increase the No. 2 frequency. Drink <a href="https://thecompleteherbalguide.com/entries/food-drink-product-reviews/pure-raspberry-leaf/">kefir</a> on its own or add it to smoothies.</p>
<h2><img data-recalc-dims="1" loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="size-full wp-image-51317 alignnone" src="https://i0.wp.com/thecompleteherbalguide.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/Almond-oil.jpg?resize=750%2C500&#038;ssl=1" alt="Almond oil" width="750" height="500" srcset="https://i0.wp.com/thecompleteherbalguide.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/Almond-oil.jpg?w=750&amp;ssl=1 750w, https://i0.wp.com/thecompleteherbalguide.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/Almond-oil.jpg?resize=300%2C200&amp;ssl=1 300w, https://i0.wp.com/thecompleteherbalguide.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/Almond-oil.jpg?resize=100%2C67&amp;ssl=1 100w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 750px) 100vw, 750px" /></h2>
<h2><span class="ez-toc-section" id="5_Almonds"></span>5. Almonds<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>
<p>Almonds are loaded with heart-healthy fats, protein, and fiber, but it&#8217;s the high magnesium content that has our intestines excited. &#8220;Magnesium neutralizes stomach acid and moves stools through the intestines,&#8221; says Morgan. And just a small handful (1 ounce) contains 25% of your daily dose. Almonds make the perfect portable snack, or you can add almond flour to baked goods and smoothies.</p>
<h2><span class="ez-toc-section" id="6_Black_beans"></span>6. Black beans<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>
<p>Just 1 cup of black beans has a whopping 15 g of fiber (<a href="https://thecompleteherbalguide.com/entries/foods-recipes/noatmeal-breakfast-bowl/">women need 25 g a day</a>), as well as magnesium and potassium for a smoother-running digestive system. Add to salads, salsas, and soups or sauté with greens.</p>
<h2><span class="ez-toc-section" id="7_Prunes"></span>7. Prunes<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>
<p>The age-old constipation cure is not only high in fiber (6 g per ½ cup), but prunes also contain dihydroxy phenyl isatin, a natural compound that stimulates the bowel, as well as sorbitol, a sugar that has a laxative effect. Plus, prunes have double the potassium of bananas. Not consuming enough potassium can cause constipation and fatigue. Chop &#8217;em up and add to salads, oatmeal, and yogurt parfaits.</p>
<h2><img data-recalc-dims="1" loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="size-full wp-image-50469 alignnone" src="https://i0.wp.com/thecompleteherbalguide.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/spinach-4.jpg?resize=750%2C500&#038;ssl=1" alt="spinach" width="750" height="500" srcset="https://i0.wp.com/thecompleteherbalguide.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/spinach-4.jpg?w=750&amp;ssl=1 750w, https://i0.wp.com/thecompleteherbalguide.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/spinach-4.jpg?resize=300%2C200&amp;ssl=1 300w, https://i0.wp.com/thecompleteherbalguide.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/spinach-4.jpg?resize=100%2C67&amp;ssl=1 100w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 750px) 100vw, 750px" /></h2>
<h2><span class="ez-toc-section" id="8_Leafy_greens"></span>8. Leafy greens<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>
<p>Spinach, Swiss chard, and kale are packed with nutrients that have poop powers, including fiber (1 cup of Swiss chard has 4 g of fiber), magnesium to help the colon contract, and potassium, which helps regulate fluid balance and muscle contractions.</p>
<h2><img data-recalc-dims="1" loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="size-full wp-image-46801 alignnone" src="https://i0.wp.com/thecompleteherbalguide.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/01/Wheat-Germ-Oil.jpg?resize=750%2C500&#038;ssl=1" alt="Wheat-Germ Oil" width="750" height="500" srcset="https://i0.wp.com/thecompleteherbalguide.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/01/Wheat-Germ-Oil.jpg?w=750&amp;ssl=1 750w, https://i0.wp.com/thecompleteherbalguide.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/01/Wheat-Germ-Oil.jpg?resize=300%2C200&amp;ssl=1 300w, https://i0.wp.com/thecompleteherbalguide.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/01/Wheat-Germ-Oil.jpg?resize=100%2C67&amp;ssl=1 100w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 750px) 100vw, 750px" /></h2>
<h2><span class="ez-toc-section" id="9_Wheat_Bran"></span>9. Wheat Bran<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>
<p>No surprise that studies show wheat bran can relieve constipation and improve digestion. The outer layer of the wheat kernel is a <a href="https://thecompleteherbalguide.com/entries/constipation/how-to-relieve-constipation-quickly-and-naturally/">fiber force</a> with a whopping 25 g per cup. Sprinkle it over your oatmeal, whip up a batch of bran muffins, or eat a bowl of All-Bran cereal.</p>
<h2><img data-recalc-dims="1" loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="size-full wp-image-52516 alignnone" src="https://i0.wp.com/thecompleteherbalguide.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/coffee-5.jpg?resize=750%2C500&#038;ssl=1" alt="coffee" width="750" height="500" srcset="https://i0.wp.com/thecompleteherbalguide.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/coffee-5.jpg?w=750&amp;ssl=1 750w, https://i0.wp.com/thecompleteherbalguide.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/coffee-5.jpg?resize=300%2C200&amp;ssl=1 300w, https://i0.wp.com/thecompleteherbalguide.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/coffee-5.jpg?resize=100%2C67&amp;ssl=1 100w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 750px) 100vw, 750px" /></h2>
<h2><span class="ez-toc-section" id="10_Coffee_tea_or_decaf"></span>10. Coffee, tea, or decaf<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>
<p>Your morning cup of Joe can get your bowels moving, but it&#8217;s not just the <a href="https://thecompleteherbalguide.com/entries/diabetes/5-best-food-choices-for-people-with-diabetes/">caffeinated stuff</a>. One study found that coffee—including decaf—means a bathroom visit for about 30% of people. Experts believe coffee&#8217;s acidity is key, notably its chlorogenic acid, a compound that gives java its bitter flavor.</p>
