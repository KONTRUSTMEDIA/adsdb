<p>Appreciative inquiry&#xA0;(AI) is a psychological approach that some businesses use to encourage positive change. If they want a better company culture, they can utilize appreciative inquiry to accomplish the goal. Likewise, if they want to improve customer satisfaction with the brand, they can use appreciative inquiry to create an effective action plan to accomplish that objective. Appreciative inquiry isn&#8217;t only for businesses either. It has been used in sports and in personal lives for triggering positive change. Below we will explain what AI is and provide helpful tips for applying it in sports.<br />
Note that this article isn&#8217;t a definitive resource on appreciative inquiry. For serious problems, it&#8217;s best to consult a professional. It&#8217;s also a good idea to continue learning more about AI before implementing it to ensure its success.</p><!-- Ezoic - wp_under_page_title - under_page_title --><div id="ezoic-pub-ad-placeholder-102" data-inserter-version="2" data-placement-location="under_page_title"></div><script data-ezoic="1">ezstandalone.cmd.push(function () { ezstandalone.showAds(102); });</script><!-- End Ezoic - wp_under_page_title - under_page_title --><p><em>*This post may contain affiliate links. <h2><span class="ez-toc-section" id="What_Is_Appreciative_Inquiry"></span>What Is Appreciative Inquiry?<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>
<p>Appreciative inquiry is an approach to change management involving identifying what&#8217;s working and why it&#8217;s working. Then, the individual does more of what&#8217;s working well. This approach is based on the belief that an organization (or in sports, a team) grows wherever the people of the organization (or the team players) focus.<br />
<figure id="attachment_3595" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-3595" style="width: 700px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img decoding="async" class="wp-image-3595" src="https://sportsandthemind.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/05/1_Appreciative-inquiry-areas-1.jpg" alt="the four areas of appreciative inquiry" width="700" height="525" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-3595" class="wp-caption-text">Image Source: <a href="https://s-media-cache-ak0.pinimg.com/736x/38/5f/48/385f489d827fd8aad4eccc17157ed57d.jpg" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Pinterest</a></figcaption></figure><br />
Part of appreciative inquiry&#8217;s process involves asking guided questions that support employee interaction with each other and positive thinking. Each question must focus on one of four areas: <a href="http://www.gervasebushe.ca/the_AI_model.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong>discover</strong>, <strong>dream</strong>, <strong>design</strong>, and <strong>deliver</strong></a>. The four key areas are also&#xA0;referred to as&#xA0;the&#xA0;4-D Model.</p><!-- Ezoic - wp_under_first_paragraph - under_first_paragraph --><div id="ezoic-pub-ad-placeholder-110" data-inserter-version="2" data-placement-location="under_first_paragraph"></div><script data-ezoic="1">ezstandalone.cmd.push(function () { ezstandalone.showAds(110); });</script><!-- End Ezoic - wp_under_first_paragraph - under_first_paragraph -->
<h2 style="text-align: center;"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="6_Tips_for_Appreciative_Inquiry"></span>6 Tips for Appreciative Inquiry<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>
<h3><span class="ez-toc-section" id="1_The_way_one_asks_questions_determines_what_they_find"></span>1. The way one asks questions determines what they find<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>
<p>This is why questions are framed positively with this approach.&#xA0;<a href="http://positivitystrategist.com/appreciative-inquiry-overview/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">One has more to gain from positive questions than negative</a>.&#xA0;According to the simultaneity principle in appreciative inquiry, inquiry and change are simultaneous. A small change occurs when one asks someone a question.</p>
<h3><span class="ez-toc-section" id="2_Its_important_to_feel_positive_in_order_to_take_positive_action"></span>2. It&#8217;s important to feel positive in order to take positive action<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>
<p>This is another principle of AI. Feeling positive emotions strengthens one&#8217;s sense of wellbeing and increases their energy. It empowers them to nurture relationships. Positive feelings cause a person to be more receptive to new ideas from others. One&#8217;s attention and thinking also expand momentarily. Ways in which one can feel positive is to think positively, watch inspiring movies, and talk about positive things. Cutting back on discussing celebrity drama and other negative news is also helpful.</p><!-- Ezoic - wp_under_second_paragraph - under_second_paragraph --><div id="ezoic-pub-ad-placeholder-111" data-inserter-version="2" data-placement-location="under_second_paragraph"></div><script data-ezoic="1">ezstandalone.cmd.push(function () { ezstandalone.showAds(111); });</script><!-- End Ezoic - wp_under_second_paragraph - under_second_paragraph -->
<h3><span class="ez-toc-section" id="3_Remember_the_importance_of_camaraderie_in_sports"></span>3. Remember the importance of camaraderie&#xA0;in sports<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>
<p><figure id="attachment_3597" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-3597" style="width: 700px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img decoding="async" class="wp-image-3597" src="https://sportsandthemind.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/05/2_young-athletes.jpg" alt="gathering hands in the huddle" width="700" height="466" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-3597" class="wp-caption-text">Image Source: <a href="https://s-media-cache-ak0.pinimg.com/736x/eb/ba/e2/ebbae27af139ae4cdcfa17c79ee4153a.jpg" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Pinterest</a></figcaption></figure><br />
However, some coaches forget the importance of camaraderie or don&#8217;t understand why it&#8217;s beneficial. The positive principle in appreciative inquiry explains that social bonding and positive affect must occur for momentum and sustainable change. Coaches that ignore camaraderie will notice change doesn&#8217;t last when applying AI.<br />
<a href="http://www.factorhappiness.at/downloads/quellen/S15_Sacharin.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Positive sentiments increase cognitive flexibility</a>, creativity, and openness to new ideas. Why is creativity important in sports? Creativity in making a goal can make the difference between winning and losing. Finding an unexpected route to landing a goal takes the other team by surprise. Examples of positive sentiments include camaraderie, hope, joy, excitement, and inspiration. Aim to create these feelings in players during their bonding time. This principle is also effective in teams in which players have conflict among each other because it improves their relations.</p>
<h3><span class="ez-toc-section" id="4_Coaches_must_be_selective_about_what_they_choose_to_believe"></span>4. Coaches must be selective about what they choose to believe<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>
<p>According to the constructionist principle, what a person believes to be true determines what they do. It also states that communication and conversation are at the core of change and organization. Also know that poor communication skills can interfere with the effectiveness of AI. <h3><span class="ez-toc-section" id="5_Start_telling_better_stories_to_the_team"></span>5. Start telling better stories to the team<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>
<p>The poetic principle of AI states that the stories people tell each other every day shape organizational life. A coach can&#8217;t completely control the stories team members tell each other. But the coach has control over the stories they tell the team. As a leader, the coach must guide players to sharing their own inspiring stories with their own. <h3><span class="ez-toc-section" id="6_Create_a_vision_with_the_team"></span>6. Create a vision with the team<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>
<p>The best companies have an inspiring vision. Having a vision statement isn&#8217;t something limited to business, however. Vision statements are helpful in personal life and in sports as well.
