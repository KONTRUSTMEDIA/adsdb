                <section id="physics" class="page">
                    <span class="tag physics">PHY 301</span>
                    <h1>Physics: Mechanics & Quantum Theory</h1>
                    
                    <h2>1. Classical Mechanics: Kinematics</h2>
                    <p>Kinematics describes the motion of points, bodies (objects), and systems of bodies without considering the forces that cause them to move.</p>
                    <div class="equation-box">
v = v₀ + at
x = x₀ + v₀t + ½at²
v² = v₀² + 2a(x - x₀)
                    </div>
                    
                    <h2>2. The Laws of Thermodynamics</h2>
                    <p>Thermodynamics deals with heat, work, and temperature, and their relation to energy, radiation, and physical properties of matter.</p>
                    <ul>
                        <li><strong>Zeroth Law:</strong> If two systems are in thermal equilibrium with a third system, they are in thermal equilibrium with each other.</li>
                        <li><strong>First Law:</strong> Energy cannot be created or destroyed in an isolated system (Conservation of Energy). <code style="background:#e2e8f0; padding:2px 4px; border-radius:4px">ΔU = Q - W</code></li>
                        <li><strong>Second Law:</strong> The entropy of any isolated system always increases.</li>
                    </ul>

                    <h2>3. Quantum Mechanics</h2>
                    <p>The study of matter and light at the atomic and subatomic scale. It attempts to describe and account for the properties of molecules and atoms and their constituents.</p>
                    <h3>The Schrödinger Equation</h3>
                    <p>A linear partial differential equation that governs the wave function of a quantum-mechanical system.</p>
                    <div class="equation-box">
iℏ ∂/∂t Ψ(r,t) = [ -ℏ²/2m ∇² + V(r,t) ] Ψ(r,t)
                    </div>
                    <p>Where <strong>Ψ</strong> is the wave function, <strong>V</strong> is the potential energy, and <strong>ℏ</strong> is the reduced Planck constant.</p>
                </section>

                    <h2>2. DNA Replication</h2>
                    <p>The biological process of producing two identical replicas of DNA from one original DNA molecule.</p>
                    <h3>Base Pairing Rules (Chargaff's Rules)</h3>
                    <p>In DNA, Adenine (A) always pairs with Thymine (T) via two hydrogen bonds, and Cytosine (C) always pairs with Guanine (G) via three hydrogen bonds.</p>
                    <div class="equation-box">
5' - A T G C C G T A - 3'  (Coding Strand)
3' - T A C G G C A T - 5'  (Template Strand)
                    </div>
                </section>

                <section id="math" class="page">
                    <span class="tag math">MAT 202</span>
                    <h1>Calculus & Linear Algebra</h1>
                    
                    <h2>1. Differential Calculus</h2>
                    <p>The derivative measures the sensitivity to change of the function value (output value) with respect to a change in its argument (input value).</p>
                    <h3>Common Derivatives</h3>
                    <ul>
                        <li><code style="font-weight:bold">d/dx (x^n) = nx^(n-1)</code> (Power Rule)</li>
                        <li><code style="font-weight:bold">d/dx (sin x) = cos x</code></li>
                        <li><code style="font-weight:bold">d/dx (e^x) = e^x</code></li>
                        <li><code style="font-weight:bold">d/dx (ln x) = 1/x</code></li>
                    </ul>

                    <h2>2. Integral Calculus</h2>
                    <p>Integration represents the accumulation of quantities, such as areas under a curve or displacement from velocity.</p>
                    <div class="equation-box">

                    <h2>2. Sorting Algorithms</h2>
                    <h3>QuickSort Implementation Logic</h3>
                    <p>QuickSort is a divide-and-conquer algorithm. It works by selecting a 'pivot' element from the array and partitioning the other elements into two sub-arrays, according to whether they are less than or greater than the pivot.</p>
                    <div class="equation-box">
function partition(arr, low, high) {
  pivot = arr[high];
  i = low - 1;
  for (j = low; j <= high - 1; j++) {
    if (arr[j] < pivot) {
      i++;
      swap(arr[i], arr[j]);
    }
  }
  swap(arr[i + 1], arr[high]);
  return i + 1;
}
