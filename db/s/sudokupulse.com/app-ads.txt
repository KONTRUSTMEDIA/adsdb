There are approximately 6.67 sextillion (6.67 × 10²¹) valid completed Sudoku grids. When you factor in symmetry, there are about 5.47 billion essentially different grids — you&rsquo;ll never run out of puzzles to solve.</p><p><strong>Is Sudoku a math game?</strong>
No. Despite using numbers, Sudoku is a logic puzzle. You could replace the digits 1–9 with letters, colors, or symbols and the game would work identically. The skill is deduction, not calculation.</p><p><strong>What&rsquo;s the minimum number of clues needed for a unique solution?</strong>
