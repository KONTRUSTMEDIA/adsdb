</header><div class="entry-content" itemprop="text"><p>The Federal Trade Commission, in an effort to protect consumers from unscrupulous advertising practices, requires bloggers to disclose monetary or goods-in-kind relationships they have with companies.</p>
<p>This is a good thing: way too many mommy bloggers were recommending amazing hair products that didn&#8217;t really work because they got a free sample in the mail, and whole swaths of readers were taken in.</p>
<p><em>Oh, the humanity!</em></p>
<p>So, my darling FTC, here&#8217;s how the compensation scheme works around here:</p>
<h2>Compensation The FTC Doesn&#8217;t Care About But I Really Do</h2>
<ul>
<li>Your comments &#8211; it&#8217;s like paying your blogger in EgoBucks. We love that.</li>
<li>Your emails &#8211; can&#8217;t always return every one but value and appreciate you for reaching out. Thank you.</li>
<li>You sharing my posts all the hell over every social media site you participate in &#8211; this is like EgoBucks with Compound Interest. We love that <em>more</em>.</li>
<li>Meeting other bloggers. Sometimes even in person &#8211; actual offline friends happen through an online community! Modern miracle.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Compensation the FTC Really Cares About and Requires I Disclose To You</h2>
<h3>Affiliate Links</h3>
<p>Some of the links out from this site are affiliate links. If you follow an affiliate link to a company or product and then end up buying something, Your price is unchanged but I get a small percentage of the sale for referring you.</p>
<p>As of September, 2019, I am an affiliate of the following companies:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="http://www.amazon.com/?_encoding=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;tag=nortediblife-20" target="_blank" rel="false noopener">Amazon.com</a> &#8211; You know them. They sell everything. As an Amazon Associate I earn from qualifying purchases.</li>
<li><a href="https://www.e-junkie.com/ecom/gb.php?cl=50607&amp;c=ib&amp;aff=208034" target="_blank" rel="false noopener">The Garden Coop</a> &#8211; A small family business out of Portland, they sell complete, downloadable plans for their attractive, secure and DIY-friendly chicken coop and run designs. Our <a href="https://nwedible.com/tag/coop" target="_blank" rel="noopener">chicken coop</a> is built on The Garden Coop plans. It&#8217;s chicken approved, kid approved and neighbor approved. We&#8217;ve been really happy with the design.</li>
</ul>
<p>I only link to things I genuinely like and use (unless I&#8217;m being ironic and linking to $2500 sunglasses). No one has any influence over the items I link to but me, and affiliate links do not impact the editorial content of this blog. Ever.</p>
<h3>Free Stuff and Giveaways</h3>
<p>People send bloggers free stuff. It&#8217;s actually one of the greatest perks to being a blogger. I get mostly books and magazine subscriptions. I am particularly partial to my free subscription to <a title="Grit Magazine" href="http://www.grit.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Grit Magazine</a>. (Update Feb 2018: currently no free Grit. Sad Panda.)</p>
<p>When I talk about something I got for free, I&#8217;ll tell you who sent it to me, and that I didn&#8217;t pay for it. Because that&#8217;s important, don&#8217;t you think?</p>
<p>Sometimes publishers or authors sponsor giveaways of their books here to get the word out about their product. I think these giveaways are great! Who wouldn&#8217;t want a chance to win free books? Sometimes, on my own dime, I self-sponsor giveaways, usually to commemorate big deal moments on the blog, like that time when I hit 1000 <a href="http://facebook.com/nwedible" target="_blank" rel="false noopener">Facebook</a> Fans. When a giveaway happens, it is always clear who is sponsoring it.</p>
<h3>Graphic and Contextual Ads</h3>
<p>Update: As of the February, 2018 redesign, there are no graphic, sponsor, or contextual ads on this site. That&#8217;s not a promise that there never will be again. But right now as I type this, NWEdible is ad-free.</p>
<p>Please consider whitelisting NWEdible in your ad blocker (and you should <em>totally</em> install an ad blocker if you don&#8217;t have one). Some of the small features of my site &#8211; like social buttons and the newsletter subscription box &#8211; disappear if you have ad block enabled even though they aren&#8217;t ads.</p>
<p>In the past, I have cautiously accepted outside sponsorship and advertising on this site. I&#8217;m proud to have had some of the most respected, ethical names in gardening, poultry keeping and food preservation as sponsors. (Shout out to <a href="http://www.highmowingseeds.com/?utm_source=NWedible&amp;utm_medium=blog&amp;utm_campaign=NWedible ">High Mowing Organic Seeds</a>, <a href="http://amzn.to/2CaFUKH" target="_blank" rel="false noopener">reCAP Mason Jars</a>, <a href="http://amzn.to/2Bj1T5f" target="_blank" rel="false noopener">Scratch and Peck Feeds</a> and <a href="http://amzn.to/2Bj2mEx" target="_blank" rel="false noopener">Mantis Garden Tools</a>.)</p>
<p>In the past I&#8217;ve placed contextual ads on my site. Contextual ads show you ads based on what <em>you&#8217;ve</em> been looking for or searching on the web. I don&#8217;t host or have access to that information. So if you see an ad for megayachts on my site, it&#8217;s because Google thinks you need a megayacht, not because I do. In fact, I don&#8217;t even know what ads you&#8217;re looking at. That megayacht is between you and your conscious.</p>
<h3>Inline Text Ads, Paid Backlinks and other Sneaky Shit</h3>
<p>There are no paid text ads or backlinks on this site and there never have been. People want to pay me to sneak text link ads into my posts <em>without you knowing they are ads</em>, and they want to pay me to write crap fake blog posts that &#8220;just happen to talk about&#8221; the product they want me to sell to you. They also want to pay me to publish the fake crap blog post <em>they</em> wrote to promote their artificial turf company&#8230;<em>or whatever</em>.</p>
<p>These requests happen all&#8230;the&#8230;time. Consider that your inside look at the underbelly of blog monetization in the real world. Gross, huh? I tell these people to go straight to spam hell, or don&#8217;t get back to them, which is basically the same thing.</p>
<h3>The Bottom Line</h3>
<p>I have spent an inordinate amount of time debating how to monetize and with whom to partner because I take my responsibility as your friendly (and trustworthy!) local blogger very seriously.</p>
<p>So if you have any questions about any of this, just <a title="Contact" href="https://nwedible.com/contact">ask</a>.  To the great chagrin of the sneaky text ad placement people, there&#8217;s nothing to hide here.</p>
<p>Thank you.</p>
