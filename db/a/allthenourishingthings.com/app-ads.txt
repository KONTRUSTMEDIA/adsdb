It’s natural, normal, and healthy to feel the full range of human emotions — angry, sad, lonely, frustrated, bored, etc. But feeling hopeful, even in troubled times, is key to getting back to that happy, content state of mind. That can mean…</span></p><ul><li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Liking who you are and feeling good in your own skin.</span></li><li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Accomplishing or working towards the goals you’ve set out for yourself.</span></li><li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Maintaining a more positive outlook on life.</span></li><li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Feeling overall satisfaction with your life.</span></li></ul><p><span style="font-weight: 400">If that seems like a tall order, it doesn’t have to be. of the biggest lessons in life is learning how to let go of what you think makes you happy and focus instead on what actually does make you happy. Think about that for a moment: if you’re trying to live someone else’s dream or live up to someone else’s expectations of you, your life will be out of balance.&nbsp;</span></p><p><span style="font-weight: 400">Landing that perfect job, buying a beautiful home, or losing 20 pounds are nice goals, but they aren’t likely to bring you actual happiness. You need to balance those things with your health, relationships, and what you find truly meaningful.&nbsp;</span></p><p><span style="font-weight: 400">The best way to figure out what’s truly important to you is to do some freewriting. Take some time and write out what you think would be your ideal life. How you spend your day, who you spend it with, how you feel, etc. Don’t limit yourself. Then, once you’ve got that down, you can start to make small changes in your life to align more closely with that ideal.&nbsp;</span></p><h3><span style="font-weight: 400">Get Outside And Get Grounded</span></h3><p><span style="font-weight: 400">Connecting with nature has always been an important key to feeling happier. So, sit down and create a daily ritual of listing the things you felt grateful for that day.&nbsp;</span></p><h3><span style="font-weight: 400">Smile More</span></h3><p><span style="font-weight: 400">It may seem like you need to feel happy in order to want to smile. But did you know you can trick your brain into thinking it’s happy by smiling?&nbsp;</span></p><p><span style="font-weight: 400">When you smile your brain releases the “happy hormone” dopamine, which helps improve your mood. Now, that doesn’t mean you have to walk around with a silly grin all day. But the next time you start feeling low, crack a smile and feel that kick of dopamine.&nbsp;</span></p><h3><span style="font-weight: 400">Go For A Walk</span></h3><p><span style="font-weight: 400">Walking daily is an easy, effective way to boost your overall happiness. Walking increases blood flow and blood circulation to the brain and body. As a result, it calms your nerves, making you feel less stressed. You could try walking on a treadmill, around a mall, or on an indoor track, but walking outdoors in nature, exposed to sunlight further enhances the benefits it has on your mood.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span></p><h3><span style="font-weight: 400">Make Sure You’re Hydrated</span></h3><p><span style="font-weight: 400">It may seem too simple but drinking water can make you feel happier. One </span><a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3984246/" target="_blank" rel="external nofollow" ><span style="font-weight: 400">study</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400"> found that increasing water intake improved mood. Drinking less water left study participants a little edgy. But increasing their water made participants naturally happier.&nbsp;So make sure you keep that water bottle full and handy all day long.&nbsp;</span></p><h3><span style="font-weight: 400">Eat For Happiness</span></h3><p><span style="font-weight: 400">Sugary, fatty, and salty foods might be your go-to for mood boosting, and it makes sense.
