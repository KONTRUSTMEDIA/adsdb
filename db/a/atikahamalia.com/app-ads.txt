		<div class="entry-content">
			<p>I was born on a bright snowy day in June, in a log cabin that I helped my father build. After 36 hours in labor with my giant head and shoulders, my mother was tired, but I was hungry.  I have pretty much stayed that way ever since.</p>
<p>My family tells stories about me only in relation to food and how much I could eat at one sitting. Needless to say, I grew to be a rather LARGE fellow so to speak. I had a driving need for foods of ALL varieties.  Food was a comfort, food was my friend. I&#8217;ve probably eaten more foods in different varieties than most people ever will&#8230;and once I found the things that satisfied the driving need for food, I learned to make or buy them&#8230;and then stuck to them and ate them in ever-increasing numbers.</p>
<p>Until it became a problem. Now&#8230;for the last year or so I&#8217;ve been working on eating better so I don&#8217;t die of a massive coronary before I&#8217;m 50. I said &#8220;better&#8221;, not perfect&#8230;if you&#8217;re looking for someone to yell at you about that double cheeseburger you like to eat&#8230;this isn&#8217;t a blog about that. It&#8217;s actually more about something I didn&#8217;t discover until last year&#8230;namely that I can eat most anything I want&#8230;and that some of the stuff I had been avoiding, was GOOD if you actually cook it right. I can make a better double cheeseburger than you&#8230;that&#8217;s better for you than anything you can buy at ANY fast food restaurant. Not only that but I found out I could eat the favorite foods&#8230;AND the good foods&#8230;and actually make myself healthier/lose weight by accident. In other words&#8230;I&#8217;ve spent the last year failing upwards.</p>
<p>So this is for the guys like me (or the girls if you are one of those) that despite knowing how to cook some food&#8230;never understood what all those extra ingredients you saw Emeril Lagasse or Bobby Flay put into dishes were for&#8230;and those who thought that if you ate healthier you had to give up your favorite foods.  YOU DON&#8217;T.</p>
<p>I&#8217;ve discovered that a man CAN cook. Simply, deliciously&#8230;and surprisingly well. And YOU can too.</p>
