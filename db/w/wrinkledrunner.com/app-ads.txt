					<p>Hey there! It seems one of our links is broken. Sorry about that!</p>
<p>Websites are tricky, and since I am a runner&#8230; not a computer expert&#8230; there are some things about blogging that are as frustrating to me as it is to click on a broken link.</p>
<p>One of the links that seem to have this trouble is our &#8220;Getting Through the Flu and Getting Back to Running&#8221; post, which you can get to <a href="https://wp.me/p9EVej-9h">here</a>.</p>
<p>If that&#8217;s not it, you can go <a href="https://wrinkledrunner.com">HERE</a> to search for another post or have a look at what we are currently up to.</p>
<p>You can  also hop over to our <a href="https://wrinkledrunner.com/start-here/">Start Here</a> page.</p>
<p>Hungry? Here is my recipe for Fish Tacos!</p>
<p>Ingredients:<br />
3 cups fresh or frozen corn kernels<br />
1 8 oz. bag angel hair coleslaw mix (regular works fine)<br />
1 fresh jalapeño- sliced and seeded, save some seeds for added heat (use gloves)<br />
2 green onions-sliced<br />
2 tomatoes- seeded and diced<br />
1 avocado- peeled, pitted and diced<br />
1/2 cups minced cilantro (more for topping)<br />
Soft taco shells (see here for homemade)<br />
Firm white fish- I used 1 filet per taco</p>
<p>Dressing for vegetables:<br />
1/4 cup lime juice<br />
3 T. honey (this is on the sweet side, use less if you’d like)<br />
1 t. sweet chili sauce<br />
1 T. Rice vinegar<br />
3 T. Olive Oil<br />
salt and ground pepper</p>
<p>Whether using fresh or frozen corn, dry fry (no oil) the kernels in a skillet (I like to use cast iron) until they are seared.</p>
<p>You can use the corn warm or cool. Toss the corn with the coleslaw mix, jalapeño (to taste), onion, tomato, avocado and cilantro.</p>
<p>Steam or bake the fish until done. I find steaming provides the best texture for the taco.</p>
<p>Whisk together the lime juice, honey, chili sauce, rice vinegar and olive oil. Add a pinch of salt and a few grinds of pepper.</p>
<p>Scoop the salad mixture on the taco shell, add fish and more cilantro.</p>
