This provides an easy way for programs to contact you in order to share credentials and information without having to access your actual email address. Programs no longer have to share credentials through spreadsheets, shared documents, or other means.</p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p> </p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-subheading intercom-interblocks-align-left"><h2 id="h_fa85d451a0">How It Works</h2></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-ordered-nested-list"><ol><li><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p>Upon creation of an account on HackerOne, the email alias will automatically generate based on the username you choose.</p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-unordered-nested-list"><ul><li><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p>Email aliases will be in the form of <b><code>[username]@wearehackerone.com</code></b></p></div></li></ul></div></li><li><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p>Programs will email you using your email alias to share special credentials or to communicate with you.</p></div></li><li><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p>The email will automatically be forwarded to your actual email address.</p></div></li></ol></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p> </p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-callout" style="background-color:#e8e8e880;border-color:#73737633"><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p><b>Note</b>: You can&#x27;t send emails with your <i>wearehackerone</i><b> </b>alias. You can only receive emails with your alias if it’s from an email address that’s not associated with your HackerOne account.</p></div></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p> </p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-subheading intercom-interblocks-align-left"><h2 id="h_f4fa8bce73">Multiple Aliases</h2></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p>You can have multiple email aliases tied to your HackerOne account. This enables you to create multiple test accounts on a program to be able to test different attack vectors on different account levels without having to create multiple HackerOne profile accounts.</p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p>You can create multiple email aliases by using <b><code>[username]+[any_identifier]@wearehackerone.com</code></b>. You can put anything you want behind the <code>+</code> sign to accommodate your needs. For instance, if you set up a test account using your default alias, <code>jobert@wearehackerone.com</code>, you can use <code>jobert+test2@wearehackerone.com</code> to create another test
