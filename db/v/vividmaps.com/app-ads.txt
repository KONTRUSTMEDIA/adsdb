<p><br>During the 2018 midterm elections in the United States, political ads varied across different <a href="https://vividmaps.com/regions-united-states/">regions</a>, reflecting the specific issues and concerns of voters in each area. Here is a general overview of some of the key topics highlighted in political ads by U.S. region:</p><div class='code-block code-block-2' style='margin: 8px 0; clear: both;'>
<div id="ezoic-pub-ad-placeholder-901"></div>

<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Midwest:</strong> Ads in the Midwest often focused on economic issues such as job creation, trade policies, and the impact of tariffs on agriculture and manufacturing industries. Healthcare was also a prominent issue, with ads discussing access to affordable healthcare and protections for pre-existing conditions.</li>



<li><strong>Northeast:</strong> In the Northeast, ads frequently addressed gun control and school safety following the Parkland school shooting earlier in the year. Climate change and environmental policies were also important topics, particularly in states like New York and Massachusetts.</li>



<li><strong>South:</strong> Political ads in the South often emphasized immigration, border security, and support for law enforcement. Some ads also highlighted the Republican-led tax reform bill and its impact on middle-class families.</li>



<li><strong>West:</strong> In the West, ads focused on a range of issues including healthcare, immigration, and the environment. There was also significant attention given to the Trump administration&#8217;s policies, with ads either supporting or criticizing the president&#8217;s agenda.</li>
</ol>



<p>Bloomberg News <a href="https://www.bloomberg.com/graphics/2018-what-the-midterm-campaign-looks-like-in-your-hometown/">examined</a> over 3 million election ads from the 2018 congressional and gubernatorial races to determine the most frequently discussed issues in 210 local television markets, as defined by the Nielsen Company. Across the United States, 16 different topics emerged as the most commonly mentioned in midterm TV ads.</p>

<p>According to Bloomberg, more than 1,200,000 TV ads have brought up health care, and nearly 75 percent of those ads have been coming from Democrat candidates.</p><div class='code-block code-block-3' style='margin: 8px 0; clear: both;'>
