<h4>Lower Your Rent Or Get Help With Buying A Home</h4>
Are you in hot water with your landlord because your rent payments are always late? Do you fear that you’ll be evicted soon since your income can’t keep up? Then applying for the Section 8 program may be a must, as it can ensure that you only pay a certain amount of your income towards the rent. If you qualify, your Section 8 voucher will go directly to the landlord, and you’ll have to pay the rest. Instead of having to spend over half of your income on rent as many low-income families do, you can reduce that amount to around 30 percent with a voucher.

If you receive a voucher, don’t feel as if you’ll be limited to living in public housing either. Not only will you get to pick the type of housing, such as an apartment, townhome, or single-family home, but you’ll also get to choose where you live.

While Section 8 can help with the rent, you may qualify for government assistance with buying a home. To learn more about these assistance programs, contact your nearest Public Housing Agency.
<h4>Get Money For Groceries</h4>
Are you having to cut back at the grocery store because your income isn’t keeping up with increasing food prices? Get grocery money from the government, and you won’t have that problem.

<a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/snap/state-directory" rel="noopener" target="_blank">A SNAP EBT card</a> can give you the power to buy groceries without paying out of pocket. The more people in your household, the more money you can receive. If you’re pregnant, breastfeeding, or have young children under the age of five, WIC can help you buy those essentials to ensure nobody’s nutrition suffers.

<h4>Get Free Education, Meals, And Healthcare For Your Kids</h4>
The high costs of raising children can make it impossible to stay afloat. There are government programs that can help, though, to take a huge financial load off your shoulders while giving your kids a steady foundation for their future.

<a href="https://eclkc.ohs.acf.hhs.gov/center-locator" rel="noopener" target="_blank">The Head Start program</a> helps children under five prepare for their education with classes, meals, health screenings, and more. That’s not the only government program for children, though, as you can find many more resources at <a href="https://www.childcare.gov/state-resources-home" rel="noopener" target="_blank">this link</a>.
<h4>Find Free Healthcare</h4>
Many people living on limited incomes let their health suffer because they cannot afford to go to the doctor, even when they’re sick. While that can save you money in the short run, you’ll eventually run into costly health issues that’ll break your bank.

<!-- Ad Slot 4 -->

To avoid this, apply for Medicaid. It offers free healthcare from the government, which has helped over 70 million people of all ages get the treatment they need without worrying about out-of-control costs.
