<div class="elementor-image"></div><h2>How to Invest in Bitcoin: A Guide to Getting Started</h2>

    <h3>Why Invest in Bitcoin?</h3>
    <p>Bitcoin has <a href="https://pocketoption.com/blog/en/post/best-way-to-invest-in-bitcoin">how to invest in bitcoin</a> become one of the most talked-about investments of the 21st century. As a decentralized digital currency, it has the potential for significant returns, and its growing acceptance by businesses and individuals is a testament to its potential. For those looking to diversify their portfolios or engage in the world of cryptocurrency, Bitcoin offers a unique opportunity.</p>

    <h3>Step 1: Choose a Bitcoin Exchange</h3>
    <p>The first step to investing in Bitcoin is selecting a reliable exchange platform. Popular options include Coinbase, Binance, and Kraken, which offer user-friendly interfaces for beginners. These platforms allow you to buy, sell, and store Bitcoin in a secure digital wallet.</p>

    <h3>Step 2: Set Up a Digital Wallet</h3>
    <p>Once you’ve chosen an exchange, you’ll need a wallet to store your Bitcoin. There are two main types of wallets: hot wallets (online) and cold wallets (offline). Hot wallets are more convenient for frequent trading, but cold wallets offer enhanced security by keeping your private keys offline.</p>

    <h3>Step 3: Deposit Funds</h3>
    <p>To buy Bitcoin, you’ll need to deposit funds into your exchange account. You can do this through bank transfers, credit cards, or other payment methods depending on the exchange. Be sure to verify your identity, as most exchanges require KYC (Know Your Customer) procedures for security.</p>

    <h3>Step 4: Make Your First Purchase</h3>
    <p>Once your account is funded, you can place an order to purchase Bitcoin. Most exchanges offer various order types, such as market orders (buying at the current market price) or limit orders (setting a price at which you're willing to buy). After completing your purchase, your Bitcoin will be transferred to your wallet.</p>

    <h3>Step 5: Monitor Your Investment</h3>
    <p>Like any investment, it’s important to regularly monitor your Bitcoin holdings. Cryptocurrency markets can be volatile, so it’s essential to stay informed about market trends and news. Many investors also use tools like price alerts to keep track of market changes.</p>

<div class="elementor-row">
<div class="elementor-element elementor-element-b0e3ecf elementor-column elementor-col-66 elementor-inner-column" data-id="b0e3ecf" data-element_type="column">
<div class="elementor-column-wrap elementor-element-populated">
<div class="elementor-widget-wrap">
<div class="elementor-element elementor-element-72437d9 elementor-widget elementor-widget-spacer" data-id="72437d9" data-element_type="widget" data-widget_type="spacer.default">

<div class="elementor-element elementor-element-afef1d3 elementor-widget elementor-widget-theme-post-content" data-id="afef1d3" data-element_type="widget" id="post-content" data-widget_type="theme-post-content.default">
<div class="elementor-widget-container">
<p>If the 20th century was the century of the skyscraper, then the 21st century is shaping up as the century of the &ldquo;plyscaper.&rdquo;</p>
<p>Despite its historical reputation for great city fires &mdash; London in 1666, the Great Chicago fire of 1871, and San Francisco in 1906 &mdash; wood is making a comeback as a construction material.</p>
<p>In Vienna, a&nbsp;275-foot building made&nbsp;almost entirely of wood has been completed. Others are in the pipeline in the rest of the world &mdash; from Canada to Australia to Europe.</p>
<p>With faster construction times and a softer environmental impact, could the building material of the past be the future of construction?</p>
<p>Vancouver-based architect Michael Green says the momentum is gaining as new engineered woods allow for greater strength and heights in buildings.</p>
<p>His firm MGA recently completed a 29.5m (97 feet) wooden building, the Wood Innovation and Design Center in Prince George, Northern British Columbia.</p>
<p>But he said news of taller wooden structures is sprouting up all the time.</p>
<div class="wp-block-image">
<figure class="aligncenter size-large"><img width="683" height="1024" src="/wp-content/uploads/2020/12/wooden_buildings_1607167721-683x1024.jpeg" alt="Wooden, Buildings" class="wp-image-8840" srcset="/wp-content/uploads/2020/12/wooden_buildings_1607167721-683x1024.jpeg 683w, /wp-content/uploads/2020/12/wooden_buildings_1607167721-200x300.jpeg 200w, /wp-content/uploads/2020/12/wooden_buildings_1607167721-768x1152.jpeg 768w, /wp-content/uploads/2020/12/wooden_buildings_1607167721-scaled.jpeg 1707w" sizes="(max-width: 683px) 100vw, 683px" />
<figcaption>Rachel Claire at Pexels</figcaption>
</figure>
</div>
<p>&ldquo;There seems to be a new announcement every two or three weeks,&rdquo; Green said. &ldquo;We&rsquo;ve got one in Vancouver for 18 stories and in Vienna, there&rsquo;s one for more than 20 stories.</p>
<p>&ldquo;We&rsquo;ve done research in high earthquake zones that show 30 stories is feasible; we certainly think we can go to 40 and higher.&rdquo;</p>
<p>He said new developments in engineered woods &mdash; small wood components that are glued together to make large panels for building &mdash; are a game-changer for construction. Mass timber panels, in particular, cross-laminated timber (known in the industry simply as CLT) are becoming established as a quicker, greener and eventually cheaper alternative to concrete and steel.</p>
<p>A great bonus of the material is the speed of construction; panels can be made to measure in the factory complete with openings, windows and doors. While the main advantages of working in wood are manifold &mdash; it&rsquo;s flexible, robust and easily worked &mdash; Green says wood may be the only material to address the growing problems of urbanization.</p>
<div class="wp-block-image">
<figure class="aligncenter size-large"><img loading="lazy" width="717" height="1024" src="/wp-content/uploads/2020/12/wooden_buildings_1607167675-717x1024.jpeg" alt="Wooden, Buildings" class="wp-image-8839" srcset="/wp-content/uploads/2020/12/wooden_buildings_1607167675-717x1024.jpeg 717w, /wp-content/uploads/2020/12/wooden_buildings_1607167675-210x300.jpeg 210w, /wp-content/uploads/2020/12/wooden_buildings_1607167675-768x1097.jpeg 768w, /wp-content/uploads/2020/12/wooden_buildings_1607167675-scaled.jpeg 1792w" sizes="(max-width: 717px) 100vw, 717px" />
<figcaption>Ben Mack at Pexels</figcaption>
</figure>
</div>
<p>&ldquo;Wood has not been an urban material so we looked at how it could be a contributor to urban environments,&rdquo; he said. &ldquo;There are a whole host of advantages.</p>
<p>&ldquo;Steel and concrete have huge carbon footprints. Concrete accounts for about 6-8 per cent of man&rsquo;s greenhouse gas emissions, whereas wood stores carbon dioxide and gives us a vehicle to create carbon-neutral buildings.&rdquo;</p>
<p>The energy used to harvest wood, he said, is much less than the enormous amount required to produce concrete and steel.</p>
<p>&ldquo;There is no other building material that is grown by the sun,&rdquo; he said. &ldquo;We&rsquo;ve calculated that the North American forests grow enough wood for a 20-story wood building every 8-10 minutes.&rdquo;</p>
<div class="wp-block-image">
<figure class="aligncenter size-large"><img loading="lazy" width="1024" height="683" src="/wp-content/uploads/2020/12/wooden_buildings_1607167761-1024x683.jpeg" alt="Wooden, Buildings" class="wp-image-8841" srcset="/wp-content/uploads/2020/12/wooden_buildings_1607167761-1024x683.jpeg 1024w, /wp-content/uploads/2020/12/wooden_buildings_1607167761-300x200.jpeg 300w, /wp-content/uploads/2020/12/wooden_buildings_1607167761-768x512.jpeg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 1024px) 100vw, 1024px" />
<figcaption>Marta Wave at Pexels</figcaption>
</figure>
</div>
<p>Ultimately, building in wood, Green argues, creates an economic incentive to plant more forests.</p>
<p>&ldquo;The climate story is really happening at both ends of the argument &mdash; by using more wood we encourage countries around the world to plant more trees.</p>
<p>&ldquo;About 20 per cent of man&rsquo;s carbon footprint comes from deforestation, this creates an important incentive for reforestation,&rdquo; he said.</p>
<p>In terms of carbon footprint, a 20-story &ldquo;plyscraper&rdquo; put against its counterpart in concrete and steel is the equivalent of taking 900 cars off the road for a year, MGA estimates.</p>
<p>Ultimately, however, buildings of the future are likely to be a mixture of wooden components and concrete and steel; combining the stability of concrete with the flexibility and speed of wood.</p>
<p>Leading timber specialist at the engineering group ARUP, Andrew Lawrence, said that shear walls &mdash; the core of tall buildings which provide stability against the lateral force of the wind or seismic activity &mdash; are likely to continue to be made from concrete and steel.</p>
<p>However, the real savings from wood come when all the cost elements of making a tall building are taken into account.</p>
<p>&ldquo;Clients are missing a trick with wood,&rdquo; he said, adding that dollar-for-dollar as a pure construction material wood can still struggle to be cheaper than concrete.</p>
<p>&ldquo;What you need to do if you want an economic wood solution is to think about all the aspects from outset.</p>
<p>&ldquo;You will save on the program because it&rsquo;s all dry and is quick to erect and potentially, if you&rsquo;re making an office building, you can leave a lot of the wood exposed, saving on the cost and time of installing finishes.&rdquo;</p>
<p>Apart from having a sustainable solution, Lawrence says, clients will gain a building that looks good too.</p>
<div class="wp-block-image">
<figure class="aligncenter size-large"><img loading="lazy" width="1024" height="684" src="/wp-content/uploads/2020/12/wooden_buildings_1607167820-1024x684.jpeg" alt="Wooden, Buildings" class="wp-image-8842" srcset="/wp-content/uploads/2020/12/wooden_buildings_1607167820-1024x684.jpeg 1024w, /wp-content/uploads/2020/12/wooden_buildings_1607167820-300x200.jpeg 300w, /wp-content/uploads/2020/12/wooden_buildings_1607167820-768x513.jpeg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 1024px) 100vw, 1024px" />
<figcaption>Eneida Nieves at Pexels</figcaption>
</figure>
</div>
<p>&ldquo;If you leave the wood exposed, you can have a really nice environment inside the building,&rdquo; he says. &ldquo;There are studies that show that people are happier inside wood structures.&rdquo;<br /></p>
</div>
