<p>Do you want to save money on your utility bills &#8211; and save the environment at the same time?</p><!-- Ezoic - wp_under_page_title - under_page_title --><div id="ezoic-pub-ad-placeholder-102" data-inserter-version="2" data-placement-location="under_page_title"></div><script data-ezoic="1">ezstandalone.cmd.push(function () { ezstandalone.showAds(102); });</script><!-- End Ezoic - wp_under_page_title - under_page_title --><p><em>*This post may contain affiliate links. <p>Energy efficient appliances are a great way to give your home a DIY upgrade. You&#x2019;ll save money in the long run, and you don&#x2019;t need to spend much up front, especially if you learn to install the appliances yourself.</p><!-- Ezoic - wp_under_first_paragraph - under_first_paragraph --><div id="ezoic-pub-ad-placeholder-110" data-inserter-version="2" data-placement-location="under_first_paragraph"></div><script data-ezoic="1">ezstandalone.cmd.push(function () { ezstandalone.showAds(110); });</script><!-- End Ezoic - wp_under_first_paragraph - under_first_paragraph -->
<p>In this handy guide, we&#x2019;ll teach you everything you need to know about installing energy efficient appliances, including how to find good deals on them. Keep reading to learn how to lower your energy bills each month.</p>
<h2><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Why_Use_Energy_Efficient_Appliances"></span>Why Use Energy Efficient Appliances?<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>
<p>There are many compelling reasons to replace your appliances with efficient ones. Let&#8217;s take a closer look at four of them.</p><!-- Ezoic - wp_under_second_paragraph - under_second_paragraph --><div id="ezoic-pub-ad-placeholder-111" data-inserter-version="2" data-placement-location="under_second_paragraph"></div><script data-ezoic="1">ezstandalone.cmd.push(function () { ezstandalone.showAds(111); });</script><!-- End Ezoic - wp_under_second_paragraph - under_second_paragraph -->
<p><span style="color: #333333; font-size: 22px;">1. You&#x2019;ll Save Money</span></p>
<p>Who wouldn&#x2019;t want lower utility bills every month? Buying energy efficient appliances is well worth it, because they&#x2019;ll quickly pay for themselves by saving you money over time.</p>
<p>Even if you pay slightly more to get them, they&#x2019;ll save you money each time you use them. Efficient appliances can save as much as <a title="" href="http://www.ase.org/resources/top-5-reasons-be-energy-efficient" rel="noopener">30 percent on your electricity bills</a>. At this rate, it doesn&#x2019;t take long for the savings to outweigh the upfront costs.</p><!-- Ezoic - wp_mid_content - mid_content --><div id="ezoic-pub-ad-placeholder-112" data-inserter-version="2" data-placement-location="mid_content"></div><script data-ezoic="1">ezstandalone.cmd.push(function () { ezstandalone.showAds(112); });</script><!-- End Ezoic - wp_mid_content - mid_content -->
<p>Of course, the amount of savings depends on the appliance you get. Your heating, ventilation, and air conditioning&#xA0; (HVAC) system takes&#xA0;up 40 percent of your energy costs. As shown in the infographic below, swapping out your HVAC gives you the most bang for the buck. However, other appliances, like washers and dryers, make a smaller but still measurable impact.</p>
<figure id="attachment_4326" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-4326" style="width: 1200px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img decoding="async" class="size-full wp-image-4326" src="https://homeprojectdiy.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/Energy-usage-stats.jpg" alt="Infographic: 40 percent of household energy costs are for heating and cooling." width="1200" height="1307" srcset="https://homeprojectdiy.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/Energy-usage-stats.jpg 1200w, https://homeprojectdiy.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/Energy-usage-stats-275x300.jpg 275w, https://homeprojectdiy.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/Energy-usage-stats-940x1024.jpg 940w, https://homeprojectdiy.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/Energy-usage-stats-768x836.jpg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 1200px) 100vw, 1200px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-4326" class="wp-caption-text">40% of the energy in your home goes to heating and cooling. 5% for cooking; 8% to refrigerators and freezers, 23% for heating water, 7% for lighting, and 14% to other. Infographic:: Choice (Australia) via <a title="" href="https://www.facebook.com/choiceaustralia/photos/a.10150141267371163.337981.181854426162/10155883514376163/?type=3" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Facebook</a>.</figcaption></figure>
<h3><span class="ez-toc-section" id="2_Raises_Your_Resale_Value"></span>2. Raises Your Resale Value<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>
<p>If you&#x2019;re using DIY projects to boost your home&#x2019;s resale value, installing energy efficient appliances is a great choice.</p>
<p>Many buyers want a home with efficient appliances. If they know they won&#x2019;t have to buy and install new appliances themselves, they&#x2019;re more likely to spring for the purchase.</p><!-- Ezoic - wp_long_content - long_content --><div id="ezoic-pub-ad-placeholder-113" data-inserter-version="2" data-placement-location="long_content"></div><script data-ezoic="1">ezstandalone.cmd.push(function () { ezstandalone.showAds(113); });</script><!-- End Ezoic - wp_long_content - long_content -->
<p><span style="color: #333333; font-size: 22px;">3. Saves the Environment</span></p>
<p>You&#x2019;ll feel good about living in a more sustainable home with energy efficient appliances. Even a single household can make a difference by making this change.</p>
<p>In fact, energy efficiency is one of the most significant ways to reduce CO2 emissions. These relatively small changes have a huge impact. Try the EPA&#x2019;s handy <a title="" href="https://www3.epa.gov/carbon-footprint-calculator/" rel="noopener">Carbon Footprint Calculator</a> to see where your household currently stands.</p>
<p><span style="color: #333333; font-size: 22px;">4. You&#x2019;ll Enjoy New Technology</span></p>
<p>When you invest in efficient appliances (even if they&#x2019;re lightly used), you&#x2019;re likely getting an upgrade from what you had before. Not only are these more efficient, but they tend to be more effective as well.</p><!-- Ezoic - wp_longer_content - longer_content --><div id="ezoic-pub-ad-placeholder-114" data-inserter-version="2" data-placement-location="longer_content"></div><script data-ezoic="1">ezstandalone.cmd.push(function () { ezstandalone.showAds(114); });</script><!-- End Ezoic - wp_longer_content - longer_content -->
<p>Efficient appliances are usually more high-tech and top-of-the-line. Efficient washers get your clothes cleaner, for example, in addition to using less energy. They&#x2019;re easier to use and can get more done in a shorter amount of time, too.</p>
<h2><span class="ez-toc-section" id="What_are_Energy_Star_Appliances"></span>What are Energy Star Appliances?<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>
<p>While researching energy efficient appliances, you&#x2019;ll likely see a lot about Energy Star appliances.</p>
<p>Energy Star appliances meet efficiency standards set by the EPA. The EPA&#x2019;s requirements are strict, so these are the most efficient appliances money can buy.</p><!-- Ezoic - wp_longest_content - longest_content --><div id="ezoic-pub-ad-placeholder-115" data-inserter-version="2" data-placement-location="longest_content"></div><script data-ezoic="1">ezstandalone.cmd.push(function () { ezstandalone.showAds(115); });</script><!-- End Ezoic - wp_longest_content - longest_content -->
<p>The Energy Star program was created to save money for households and businesses, as well as help save the environment. The EPA established the program in 1992. It&#x2019;s basically a voluntary labeling program: if an appliance meets efficiency standards, manufacturers can choose to put the Energy Star logo on it. This helps them sell more appliances while ensuring that customers get the efficiency they want.</p>
<p>When shopping for energy-efficient appliances, look for the Energy Star logo. This logo has become synonymous with efficiency and shows that the appliance meets the highest standards.</p>
<figure id="attachment_4327" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-4327" style="width: 1200px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img decoding="async" class="size-full wp-image-4327" src="https://homeprojectdiy.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/Energy-star-appliances.jpg" alt="Infographic on ways to save with Energy Star appliances and electronics. <h2><span class="ez-toc-section" id="How_to_Save_Money_with_Energy_Star_Appliances"></span>How to Save Money with Energy Star Appliances<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>
<p>You don&#8217;t have to break the bank buying energy-efficient appliances. Here are a few ideas to save money.</p><!-- Ezoic - wp_incontent_5 - incontent_5 --><div id="ezoic-pub-ad-placeholder-116" data-inserter-version="2" data-placement-location="incontent_5"></div><script data-ezoic="1">ezstandalone.cmd.push(function () { ezstandalone.showAds(116); });</script><!-- End Ezoic - wp_incontent_5 - incontent_5 -->
<p>One of the best ways to save money is to start by replacing only the appliances that will save you the most on your energy bill. Let the savings accumulate as you pay your energy bills month by month. Then, you can invest those savings into more energy efficient appliances later on.</p>
<p>Energy Star appliances do tend to cost more than non-efficient appliances. If you already have functioning appliances that aren&#x2019;t efficient, the cheapest option is usually to wait until they break down before replacing them with efficient ones.</p>
<p>You can save more money by making additional changes to your home. For example, an efficient HVAC system will work even better if you also caulk and weatherstrip the house.</p><!-- Ezoic - wp_incontent_6 - incontent_6 --><div id="ezoic-pub-ad-placeholder-117" data-inserter-version="2" data-placement-location="incontent_6"></div><script data-ezoic="1">ezstandalone.cmd.push(function () { ezstandalone.showAds(117); });</script><!-- End Ezoic - wp_incontent_6 - incontent_6 -->
<h2><span class="ez-toc-section" id="How_to_Save_Money_on_Energy_Star_Appliances"></span>How to Save Money <em>on</em> Energy Star Appliances<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>
<p>Try watching the clearance areas of your favorite retailers if you aren&#8217;t too picky about color or style. Especially if you don&#8217;t mind a ding or dent. Places like Lowe&#8217;s have a <a title="" href="https://www.lowes.com/pl/Clearance-items/4294684028?catalog=4294937007">clearance section</a> online. Home Depot has a section of their website featuring <a title="" href="https://www.homedepot.com/c/Appliance_Savings">appliances </a>you can save on. Best Buy also sells <a href="https://www.bestbuy.com/site/searchpage.jsp?cp=1&amp;searchType=search&amp;_dyncharset=UTF-8&amp;ks=960&amp;sc=Global&amp;list=y&amp;usc=All%20Categories&amp;type=page&amp;id=pcat17071&amp;iht=n&amp;seeAll=&amp;browsedCategory=pcmcat748300667606&amp;st=categoryid%24abcat0900000&amp;qp=currentoffers_facet%3DCurrent%20Offers~Outlet%20Items%5Econdition_facet%3DCondition~Open-Box&amp;sp=-bestsellingsort%20skuidsaas">open box</a> and <a title="" href="https://www.bestbuy.com/site/searchpage.jsp?cp=1&amp;searchType=search&amp;_dyncharset=UTF-8&amp;ks=960&amp;sc=Global&amp;list=y&amp;usc=All%20Categories&amp;type=page&amp;id=pcat17071&amp;iht=n&amp;seeAll=&amp;browsedCategory=pcmcat748300667606&amp;st=categoryid%24abcat0900000&amp;qp=currentoffers_facet%3DCurrent%20Offers~Outlet%20Items%5Ecurrentoffers_facet%3DCurrent%20Deals~Clearance&amp;sp=-bestsellingsort%20skuidsaas">clearance </a>appliances.</p>
<p>You can also call your local home improvement or appliance store and ask if they have any scratched or dented energy efficient appliances to replace your old, inefficient ones.</p>
<p>Finally, keep in mind that more expensive doesn&#x2019;t always mean more efficient. You can shop in the lower price range of Energy Star appliances and still get the value of energy savings.</p><!-- Ezoic - wp_incontent_7 - incontent_7 --><div id="ezoic-pub-ad-placeholder-118" data-inserter-version="2" data-placement-location="incontent_7"></div><script data-ezoic="1">ezstandalone.cmd.push(function () { ezstandalone.showAds(118); });</script><!-- End Ezoic - wp_incontent_7 - incontent_7 -->
<h2><span class="ez-toc-section" id="How_to_Install_Energy_Efficient_Appliances"></span>How to Install Energy Efficient Appliances<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>
<p>Appliances cost money, but you can save more with DIY installation. Fortunately, it&#x2019;s not hard to install many basic home appliances on your own.</p>
<p>If your home has built-in appliances (such as a dishwasher), size is the first thing you&#x2019;ll want to consider. The most efficient appliance will do you no good if it doesn&#x2019;t fit!</p>
<p>Measure the appliances you currently have, and measure the size of the openings for them before choosing your new energy efficient appliances. It&#x2019;s often best to look for a newer Energy Star version of the appliance you already have. That way, you&#x2019;ll likely find one in the same size.</p><!-- Ezoic - wp_incontent_8 - incontent_8 --><div id="ezoic-pub-ad-placeholder-119" data-inserter-version="2" data-placement-location="incontent_8"></div><script data-ezoic="1">ezstandalone.cmd.push(function () { ezstandalone.showAds(119); });</script><!-- End Ezoic - wp_incontent_8 - incontent_8 -->
<p>While shopping, you may also notice that some appliance retailers offer free installation. If so, take advantage!</p>
<p>Once you&#x2019;ve bought the new appliances and had them delivered, it&#x2019;s time to install them. Let&#x2019;s take a look at the process, step by step.</p>
<figure id="attachment_3991" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-3991" style="width: 960px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><a href="https://homeprojectdiy.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/02/appliance.jpg"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="wp-image-3991 size-full" src="https://homeprojectdiy.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/02/appliance.jpg" alt="Image of appliances." width="960" height="640" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-3991" class="wp-caption-text">Image CC by 0, by&#xA0;<a class="hover_opacity" title="" href="https://pixabay.com/en/users/PhotoMIX-Company-1546875/" rel="noopener">PhotoMIX-Company</a>&#xA0;via Pixabay.</figcaption></figure>
<h3><span class="ez-toc-section" id="1_Cut_the_Power"></span><span style="color: #333333; font-size: 22px;">1. Cut the Power</span><span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>
<p>Avoid electrocution by cutting the power to the old appliances before you get started. Each appliance typically has its own breaker.</p><!-- Ezoic - wp_incontent_9 - incontent_9 --><div id="ezoic-pub-ad-placeholder-120" data-inserter-version="2" data-placement-location="incontent_9"></div><script data-ezoic="1">ezstandalone.cmd.push(function () { ezstandalone.showAds(120); });</script><!-- End Ezoic - wp_incontent_9 - incontent_9 -->
<h3><span class="ez-toc-section" id="2_Disconnect_Appliances"></span>2. Disconnect Appliances<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>
<p>Besides the important step of cutting the power, there are other safety precautions you should take when disconnecting old appliances.</p>
<p>Wear thick work gloves to protect your hands. Pad your floors so they aren&#x2019;t scraped or dented by heavy appliances.</p>
<p>While disconnecting the electrical connections, pay attention so you know how to reconnect the new appliances. Some appliances with an electrical connection have four wires: black, red, white, and green. Other appliances simply plug into a wall outlet.</p><!-- Ezoic - wp_incontent_10 - incontent_10 --><div id="ezoic-pub-ad-placeholder-121" data-inserter-version="2" data-placement-location="incontent_10"></div><script data-ezoic="1">ezstandalone.cmd.push(function () { ezstandalone.showAds(121); });</script><!-- End Ezoic - wp_incontent_10 - incontent_10 -->
<p>To remove the electrical, you may need to remove an electrical box cover or clamp and use pliers to carefully disconnect the wires.</p>
<p>Certain types of appliances will have certain types of connectors. For example, if you&#x2019;re replacing a range top oven, you&#x2019;ll need to disconnect the exhaust duct from the exhaust system.</p>
<h3><span class="ez-toc-section" id="3_Remove_Appliances"></span><span style="color: #333333; font-size: 22px;">3. Remove Appliances</span><span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>
<p>Once everything&#x2019;s been disconnected, it&#x2019;s time to remove your old appliances.</p><!-- Ezoic - wp_incontent_11 - incontent_11 --><div id="ezoic-pub-ad-placeholder-122" data-inserter-version="2" data-placement-location="incontent_11"></div><script data-ezoic="1">ezstandalone.cmd.push(function () { ezstandalone.showAds(122); });</script><!-- End Ezoic - wp_incontent_11 - incontent_11 -->
<p>Some will be light enough for one person to handle. However, most common appliances such as ovens and dryers are too heavy for one person. Enlist the help of a family member or friend for this step &#8211; otherwise, you risk injuring yourself.</p>
<h3><span class="ez-toc-section" id="4_Install_New_Appliances"></span><span style="color: #333333; font-size: 22px;">4. Install New Appliances</span><span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>
<p>Now, take a moment to clean out the spaces where the old appliances were. This may be your only chance for a long time to get in there and remove dust, dirt, and debris (and maybe even pick up some loose change).</p>
<p>Place the new appliances where they belong. Connect everything that&#8217;s necessary, such as electrical wiring and exhaust ducts. If you pay careful attention while disconnecting the old appliances, and follow any instructions the new ones come with, this step should be easy.</p><!-- Ezoic - wp_incontent_12 - incontent_12 --><div id="ezoic-pub-ad-placeholder-123" data-inserter-version="2" data-placement-location="incontent_12"></div><script data-ezoic="1">ezstandalone.cmd.push(function () { ezstandalone.showAds(123); });</script><!-- End Ezoic - wp_incontent_12 - incontent_12 -->
<p>Now, you can enjoy your new energy efficient appliances and save money with every use.</p>
