<h2 class="gb-text gb-text-f77ab8f7"><strong>What is a Linear Foot?</strong></h2>



<p class="gb-text gb-text-57dbc985">A&nbsp;<strong>linear foot</strong>&nbsp;measures straight-line distance — it’s simply one foot in length, equivalent to&nbsp;<strong>12 inches</strong>&nbsp;or&nbsp;<strong>30.48 centimeters</strong>. Unlike&nbsp;<strong>square feet</strong>, which measure area, or&nbsp;<strong>cubic feet</strong>, which measure volume, a linear foot only measures&nbsp;<strong>one dimension: length</strong>.</p>



<p class="gb-text gb-text-6402282b">In practice, any object measured end to end — such as a piece of lumber or pipe — uses linear feet. The width or thickness doesn’t affect the measurement.</p>



<h3 class="gb-text gb-text-d489fe1b"><strong>Example:</strong></h3>



<p class="gb-text gb-text-985f4e31">If a board measures 6 feet long, it equals&nbsp;<strong>6 linear feet</strong>, regardless of whether it’s two inches or eight inches wide.</p>

<h2 class="gb-text gb-text-e14171fb"><strong>How to Calculate Linear Feet</strong></h2>



<p class="gb-text gb-text-7ab578ed">Calculating linear feet manually is simple, but prone to errors during large projects. Here’s how it works:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Measure the total length</strong>&nbsp;in inches.</li>



<li><strong>Divide the total by 12</strong>&nbsp;to convert inches into feet.
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>For example,&nbsp;<strong>96 inches ÷ 12 = 8 linear feet</strong>.</li>
</ul>
</li>



<li><strong>Sum up all lengths</strong>&nbsp;if you have multiple pieces or sections.</li>
</ul>



<p class="gb-text gb-text-60d78d70">Our&nbsp;<strong>online calculator</strong>&nbsp;automates this process in seconds. Just input your measurements, and it instantly provides accurate linear footage — so you can confidently order the exact quantity of materials you need.</p>

<h2 class="gb-text gb-text-4ea05a1f"><strong>FAQs About Linear Feet Calculators</strong></h2>



<div class="gb-element-42eae95d"><div class="wp-block-ub-content-toggle wp-block-ub-content-toggle-block" id="ub-content-toggle-block-155727b9-3814-4362-b317-64b9ec056614" data-mobilecollapse="false" data-desktopcollapse="false" data-preventcollapse="false" data-showonlyone="true">
<div class="wp-block-ub-content-toggle-accordion" style="border-color: #f1f1f1; " id="ub-content-toggle-panel-block-">
			<div class="wp-block-ub-content-toggle-accordion-title-wrap" style="background-color: #f1f1f1;" aria-controls="ub-content-toggle-panel-0-155727b9-3814-4362-b317-64b9ec056614" tabindex="0">
			<p class="wp-block-ub-content-toggle-accordion-title ub-content-toggle-title-155727b9-3814-4362-b317-64b9ec056614" style="color: #000000; "><strong><strong>1. What is the difference between linear feet and square feet?</strong></strong></p>
			<div class="wp-block-ub-content-toggle-accordion-toggle-wrap right" style="color: #000000;"><span class="wp-block-ub-content-toggle-accordion-state-indicator wp-block-ub-chevron-down open"></span></div>
		</div>
			<div role="region" aria-expanded="true" class="wp-block-ub-content-toggle-accordion-content-wrap" id="ub-content-toggle-panel-0-155727b9-3814-4362-b317-64b9ec056614">

<p>Linear feet measure length in one direction, while square feet measure area (length × width).</p>

</div>
		</div>

<div class="wp-block-ub-content-toggle-accordion" style="border-color: #f1f1f1; " id="ub-content-toggle-panel-block-">
			<div class="wp-block-ub-content-toggle-accordion-title-wrap" style="background-color: #f1f1f1;" aria-controls="ub-content-toggle-panel-1-155727b9-3814-4362-b317-64b9ec056614" tabindex="0">
			<p class="wp-block-ub-content-toggle-accordion-title ub-content-toggle-title-155727b9-3814-4362-b317-64b9ec056614" style="color: #000000; "><strong><strong>2. How many inches are in one linear foot?</strong></strong></p>
			<div class="wp-block-ub-content-toggle-accordion-toggle-wrap right" style="color: #000000;"><span class="wp-block-ub-content-toggle-accordion-state-indicator wp-block-ub-chevron-down"></span></div>
		</div>
			<div role="region" aria-expanded="false" class="wp-block-ub-content-toggle-accordion-content-wrap ub-hide" id="ub-content-toggle-panel-1-155727b9-3814-4362-b317-64b9ec056614">

<p>One linear foot equals 12 inches.</p>

</div>
		</div>

<div class="wp-block-ub-content-toggle-accordion" style="border-color: #f1f1f1; " id="ub-content-toggle-panel-block-">
			<div class="wp-block-ub-content-toggle-accordion-title-wrap" style="background-color: #f1f1f1;" aria-controls="ub-content-toggle-panel-2-155727b9-3814-4362-b317-64b9ec056614" tabindex="0">
			<p class="wp-block-ub-content-toggle-accordion-title ub-content-toggle-title-155727b9-3814-4362-b317-64b9ec056614" style="color: #000000; "><strong><strong>3. How do I convert square feet to linear feet?</strong></strong></p>
			<div class="wp-block-ub-content-toggle-accordion-toggle-wrap right" style="color: #000000;"><span class="wp-block-ub-content-toggle-accordion-state-indicator wp-block-ub-chevron-down"></span></div>
		</div>
			<div role="region" aria-expanded="false" class="wp-block-ub-content-toggle-accordion-content-wrap ub-hide" id="ub-content-toggle-panel-2-155727b9-3814-4362-b317-64b9ec056614">

<p>You can’t directly convert square feet to linear feet without knowing the width of the material. For example, if flooring is 2 feet wide, divide total square feet by the width (e.g., 100 sq. ft ÷ 2 ft = 50 linear feet).</p>

</div>
		</div>

<div class="wp-block-ub-content-toggle-accordion" style="border-color: #f1f1f1; " id="ub-content-toggle-panel-block-">
			<div class="wp-block-ub-content-toggle-accordion-title-wrap" style="background-color: #f1f1f1;" aria-controls="ub-content-toggle-panel-3-155727b9-3814-4362-b317-64b9ec056614" tabindex="0">
			<p class="wp-block-ub-content-toggle-accordion-title ub-content-toggle-title-155727b9-3814-4362-b317-64b9ec056614" style="color: #000000; "><strong><strong>4. Can I use this calculator for irregular shapes?</strong></strong></p>
			<div class="wp-block-ub-content-toggle-accordion-toggle-wrap right" style="color: #000000;"><span class="wp-block-ub-content-toggle-accordion-state-indicator wp-block-ub-chevron-down"></span></div>
		</div>
			<div role="region" aria-expanded="false" class="wp-block-ub-content-toggle-accordion-content-wrap ub-hide" id="ub-content-toggle-panel-3-155727b9-3814-4362-b317-64b9ec056614">

<p>Yes. Measure each side separately and add them together before entering the total length into the calculator.</p>

</div>
		</div>

<div class="wp-block-ub-content-toggle-accordion" style="border-color: #f1f1f1; " id="ub-content-toggle-panel-block-">
			<div class="wp-block-ub-content-toggle-accordion-title-wrap" style="background-color: #f1f1f1;" aria-controls="ub-content-toggle-panel-4-155727b9-3814-4362-b317-64b9ec056614" tabindex="0">
			<p class="wp-block-ub-content-toggle-accordion-title ub-content-toggle-title-155727b9-3814-4362-b317-64b9ec056614" style="color: #000000; "><strong><strong>5. Why is linear measurement important in shipping?</strong></strong></p>
			<div class="wp-block-ub-content-toggle-accordion-toggle-wrap right" style="color: #000000;"><span class="wp-block-ub-content-toggle-accordion-state-indicator wp-block-ub-chevron-down open"></span></div>
		</div>
			<div role="region" aria-expanded="true" class="wp-block-ub-content-toggle-accordion-content-wrap" id="ub-content-toggle-panel-4-155727b9-3814-4362-b317-64b9ec056614">

<p>Carriers often use linear feet to determine how much space your shipment takes in a trailer or container, affecting cost.</p>
