<h3 class="rank-math-question "><strong>Why Learn About Power and Money?</strong></h3>
<div class="rank-math-answer ">
<img decoding="async" width="150" height="150" src="data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%20150%20150'%3E%3C/svg%3E" data-src="https://www.lrnin.in/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/lakshmi-ji-vishnu-ji-photos-150x150.jpg" class="alignright lazy" alt="Lakshmi ji &amp; Vishnu ji photos" title="Learning | Lrnin 1">
<p>Knowing the true nature of <strong>power</strong> and <strong>money</strong> helps us make rational decisions and accept outcomes with clarity. Power <strong>creates</strong> money; money only <strong>supports</strong> power. It may mimic power but lacks true authority unless supported by strength or influence.<br />🔍 <em>Example from Hindu mythology:</em><br />Lord Vishnu (symbolizing power) and Goddess Lakshmi (representing wealth) together show the synergy—where true wealth follows real power.</p>

</div>

<div id="faq-question-1724225705055" class="rank-math-list-item">
<h3 class="rank-math-question ">What is Power?</h3>
<div class="rank-math-answer ">

<p>Power is the ability to act, influence, and lead. It can be achieved through:<br />Leadership<br />Merit<br />Skills<br />Fairness<br />Strength<br />Dedication<br />Discipline etc<br />True power is earned and sustained—not bought.</p>

</div>
</div>
<div id="faq-question-1724226932891" class="rank-math-list-item">
<h3 class="rank-math-question ">What is Money?</h3>
<div class="rank-math-answer ">

<p>Money is a <strong>human-made agreement</strong> to exchange value. Unlike the universal barter system found in nature, money is uniquely human and evolves with society. It works based on <strong>demand and supply</strong> and takes various forms—cash, digital assets, bullion, and more.</p>

</div>
</div>
<div id="faq-question-1724231215127" class="rank-math-list-item">
<h3 class="rank-math-question ">What Role Does Time Play?</h3>
<div class="rank-math-answer ">

<p><strong>Time is the context</strong> in which power and money operate. Over time, these elements shift, evolve, and change hands. Understanding time helps us make sense of economic cycles, investment decisions, and life transitions.</p>

</div>
</div>
</div>
</div>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Learning about Savings</h2>



<p>The concept of savings dates back to <strong>10000 BC</strong> when humans began the practice of <strong>farming</strong>. The saving for the future was done by storing grains in pots &amp; granaries. This also led to the development of <strong>pottery</strong>. In modern times <strong>pots can be equated with banks</strong>. Banks hold money but they are also engaged in activity that lead to creation of money.</p>



<p>Slowly settlements began to exchange goods within themselves &amp; with other settlements. This system of exchange of goods was called the <strong>barter system</strong>. The barter system worked on the principle of <strong>double coincidence of wants</strong>. This restricted the <strong>scale </strong>of exchange or trade. However, trade continued in relatively small quantities based on the <strong>needs</strong>. As the population increased this activity picked up the pace.</p>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><a href="https://in.pinterest.com/pin/333407178660010937/" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><img decoding="async" width="1017" height="605" src="data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%201017%20605'%3E%3C/svg%3E" data-src="https://www.lrnin.in/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/ancient-money.png" alt="Ancient forms of Money" class="wp-image-8763 lazy" title="Learning | Lrnin 5" data-srcset="https://www.lrnin.in/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/ancient-money.png 1017w, https://www.lrnin.in/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/ancient-money-300x178.png 300w, https://www.lrnin.in/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/ancient-money-768x457.png 768w, https://www.lrnin.in/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/ancient-money-150x89.png 150w" data-sizes="auto, (max-width: 1017px) 100vw, 1017px" /></a><figcaption class="wp-element-caption">Ancient Money Image source Pinterest</figcaption></figure>



<p>To overcome this hurdle (the double coincidence of wants) the mathematical concept of <strong>Money </strong>was invented. <strong>Money </strong>always has an <strong>issuer </strong>be it ancient times or modern times. An issuer is always a<strong> powerful person</strong>, <strong>entity, institution, or nation</strong>. In earlier times whenever the issuer of the money became weak due to any reason. The money took the form of a precious, scarce, limited natural resource that is durable, valuable &amp; universally acceptable. Hence we see bullion metals like Gold, Silver, and Platinum taking the form of money.  An important learning from this paragraph is that during a time of crisis, <strong>bullion </strong>will have more value than other forms of money. </p>



<p>All of this resulted in, trades that were <strong>bigger in size</strong> can be executed &amp; settled easily. It is important to know all this to expand the scope of our learning. Money derived its purchasing power from the <strong>issuer</strong>, and the exchanges, the issuer is usually a powerful person or institution or a country. Since there were many issuers in the past like we have many currencies today. It created a unique problem of <strong>standardization of money</strong>. <strong>Gold </strong>was used to overcome this problem.</p>



<p>In <strong>peacetime </strong>and in times of <strong>prosperity</strong>, anything can acquire the form of money if the <strong>issuer is powerful and strong</strong> enough to win the trust and enforce the obligation that comes upon issuance and circulations. The relative strength of the issuer gave rise to a system of currencies in modern times.</p>
