                    <div class="faqs-contents"><h2 class="font">Crypto Basics</h2><p><p>Understanding the fundamentals of cryptocurrency is the first step for anyone looking to enter the world of digital assets. This section covers what cryptocurrencies are, how they work, and the key differences between various types of digital currencies.</p></p><h3 class="adn-title" id="title-faq-1-1" role="button" aria-controls="content-faq-1-1" aria-expanded="false">What is a cryptocurrency?</h3><div class="adn-content" id="content-faq-1-1" role="region" aria-labelledby="title-faq-1-1" hidden><p><p>A cryptocurrency is a digital or virtual currency that uses cryptography for security and operates independently of any central authority. It enables peer-to-peer transactions via decentralized networks, usually built on blockchain technology.</p></p></div><h3 class="adn-title" id="title-faq-1-2" role="button" aria-controls="content-faq-1-2" aria-expanded="false">How does a cryptocurrency work?</h3><div class="adn-content" id="content-faq-1-2" role="region" aria-labelledby="title-faq-1-2" hidden><p><p>Cryptocurrencies work through a decentralized ledger system known as a blockchain. Every transaction is verified by network participants (called nodes or miners), recorded on the blockchain, and made publicly available, ensuring transparency and immutability.</p></p></div><h3 class="adn-title" id="title-faq-1-3" role="button" aria-controls="content-faq-1-3" aria-expanded="false">What is blockchain and how is it connected with cryptocurrency?</h3><div class="adn-content" id="content-faq-1-3" role="region" aria-labelledby="title-faq-1-3" hidden><p><p>Blockchain is the foundational technology behind most cryptocurrencies. It is a distributed ledger that records transactions in a secure, tamper-proof way. Cryptocurrencies use blockchains to maintain a transparent and decentralized record of transactions.</p></p></div><h3 class="adn-title" id="title-faq-1-4" role="button" aria-controls="content-faq-1-4" aria-expanded="false">Who invented cryptocurrency?</h3><div class="adn-content" id="content-faq-1-4" role="region" aria-labelledby="title-faq-1-4" hidden><p><p>The first cryptocurrency, Bitcoin, was invented in 2008 by a person or group of people using the pseudonym Satoshi Nakamoto. The true identity remains unknown, but Nakamoto published the Bitcoin white paper and launched the network in 2009.</p></p></div><h3 class="adn-title" id="title-faq-1-5" role="button" aria-controls="content-faq-1-5" aria-expanded="false">What is the difference between Bitcoin and altcoins?</h3><div class="adn-content" id="content-faq-1-5" role="region" aria-labelledby="title-faq-1-5" hidden><p><p>Bitcoin is the first and most well-known cryptocurrency. Altcoins refer to any cryptocurrency that is not Bitcoin. While some altcoins aim to improve upon Bitcoin’s technology, others are designed for entirely different use cases, such as smart contracts or stable digital payments.</p></p></div><h3 class="adn-title" id="title-faq-1-6" role="button" aria-controls="content-faq-1-6" aria-expanded="false">What is the difference between a token and a coin?</h3><div class="adn-content" id="content-faq-1-6" role="region" aria-labelledby="title-faq-1-6" hidden><p><p>Coins operate on their own blockchain (e.g., Bitcoin or Ethereum), while tokens are built on existing blockchains (e.g., ERC-20 tokens on Ethereum). Coins typically serve as a medium of exchange, while tokens can represent assets, access, or utility.</p></p></div></div></div></div><div class="faqs-destacadas faqs-index" id="acordeon-2">
                <div class="wrapper faqs">
                    <div class="faqs-contents"><h2 class="font">Crypto Economics</h2><p><p>This section explains how the value of cryptocurrencies is determined, what market cap means, and why circulating supply matters for understanding the true scale and potential of a digital asset.</p></p><h3 class="adn-title" id="title-faq-2-1" role="button" aria-controls="content-faq-2-1" aria-expanded="false">How are crypto prices calculated?</h3><div class="adn-content" id="content-faq-2-1" role="region" aria-labelledby="title-faq-2-1" hidden><p><p>Crypto prices are determined by supply and demand on cryptocurrency exchanges. Prices can vary slightly between exchanges but are typically averaged across markets based on trading volume.</p></p></div><h3 class="adn-title" id="title-faq-2-2" role="button" aria-controls="content-faq-2-2" aria-expanded="false">What is the market cap of cryptocurrency?</h3><div class="adn-content" id="content-faq-2-2" role="region" aria-labelledby="title-faq-2-2" hidden><p><p>Market capitalization, or market cap, is the total value of a cryptocurrency. It is calculated by multiplying the current price per coin by the circulating supply of that coin.</p></p></div><h3 class="adn-title" id="title-faq-2-3" role="button" aria-controls="content-faq-2-3" aria-expanded="false">Does market cap matter in cryptocurrency?</h3><div class="adn-content" id="content-faq-2-3" role="region" aria-labelledby="title-faq-2-3" hidden><p><p>Yes, market cap helps investors assess a cryptocurrency’s relative size, stability, and potential risk. Larger market cap assets like Bitcoin and Ethereum are generally considered less volatile than smaller-cap altcoins.</p></p></div><h3 class="adn-title" id="title-faq-2-4" role="button" aria-controls="content-faq-2-4" aria-expanded="false">How is crypto market cap calculated?</h3><div class="adn-content" id="content-faq-2-4" role="region" aria-labelledby="title-faq-2-4" hidden><p><p>Market cap = Current price × Circulating supply. For example, if a coin costs $100 and there are 10 million coins in circulation, the market cap is $1 billion.</p></p></div><h3 class="adn-title" id="title-faq-2-5" role="button" aria-controls="content-faq-2-5" aria-expanded="false">How can a cryptocurrency increase its market cap?</h3><div class="adn-content" id="content-faq-2-5" role="region" aria-labelledby="title-faq-2-5" hidden><p><p>A cryptocurrency can increase its market cap by either rising in price, expanding its circulating supply, or both. Increased adoption, investor confidence, and real-world use cases often drive price growth.</p></p></div><h3 class="adn-title" id="title-faq-2-6" role="button" aria-controls="content-faq-2-6" aria-expanded="false">What is Bitcoin’s market cap?</h3><div class="adn-content" id="content-faq-2-6" role="region" aria-labelledby="title-faq-2-6" hidden><p><p>Bitcoin’s market cap fluctuates based on its current trading price and circulating supply. It has consistently held the largest market cap in the crypto industry, often exceeding hundreds of billions of dollars.</p></p></div><h3 class="adn-title" id="title-faq-2-7" role="button" aria-controls="content-faq-2-7" aria-expanded="false">What is cryptocurrency circulating supply?</h3><div class="adn-content" id="content-faq-2-7" role="region" aria-labelledby="title-faq-2-7" hidden><p><p>Circulating supply refers to the number of cryptocurrency units that are currently available and in circulation among the public. It does not include coins that are locked, burned, or reserved for future use.</p></p></div></div></div></div><div class="faqs-destacadas faqs-index" id="acordeon-3">
                <div class="wrapper faqs">
                    <div class="faqs-contents"><h2 class="font">Buying and Investing</h2><p><p>If you're considering investing in cryptocurrencies, it's essential to understand the different types of assets, how to choose wisely, and the steps to purchase and store your coins safely.</p></p><h3 class="adn-title" id="title-faq-3-1" role="button" aria-controls="content-faq-3-1" aria-expanded="false">Which is the best cryptocurrency?</h3><div class="adn-content" id="content-faq-3-1" role="region" aria-labelledby="title-faq-3-1" hidden><p><p>There is no single “best” cryptocurrency, as it depends on your goals. Bitcoin is the most established, Ethereum is known for smart contracts, and other altcoins offer various features. Research and risk tolerance are key factors in your decision.</p></p></div><h3 class="adn-title" id="title-faq-3-2" role="button" aria-controls="content-faq-3-2" aria-expanded="false">What cryptocurrency should I buy?</h3><div class="adn-content" id="content-faq-3-2" role="region" aria-labelledby="title-faq-3-2" hidden><p><p>It depends on your investment strategy. Beginners often start with Bitcoin or Ethereum. Consider factors like project fundamentals, market cap, utility, community support, and development activity before investing.</p></p></div><h3 class="adn-title" id="title-faq-3-3" role="button" aria-controls="content-faq-3-3" aria-expanded="false">What are the top 10 cryptocurrencies?</h3><div class="adn-content" id="content-faq-3-3" role="region" aria-labelledby="title-faq-3-3" hidden><p><p>The top 10 cryptocurrencies by market cap often include Bitcoin (BTC), Ethereum (ETH), Binance Coin (BNB), Solana (SOL), XRP, Cardano (ADA), Dogecoin (DOGE), Toncoin (TON), Avalanche (AVAX), and Polkadot (DOT). Rankings can change over time.</p></p></div><h3 class="adn-title" id="title-faq-3-4" role="button" aria-controls="content-faq-3-4" aria-expanded="false">How can I buy a coin I like?</h3><div class="adn-content" id="content-faq-3-4" role="region" aria-labelledby="title-faq-3-4" hidden><p><p>You can buy cryptocurrencies on exchanges like Coinbase, Binance, or Kraken. After creating an account and verifying your identity, you can deposit funds and purchase your desired coin using fiat or other crypto assets.</p></p></div><h3 class="adn-title" id="title-faq-3-5" role="button" aria-controls="content-faq-3-5" aria-expanded="false">What is a stablecoin?</h3><div class="adn-content" id="content-faq-3-5" role="region" aria-labelledby="title-faq-3-5" hidden><p><p>A stablecoin is a type of cryptocurrency that is pegged to a stable asset, such as the US dollar. Examples include USDT (Tether), USDC, and DAI. They are commonly used for trading, remittances, and as a hedge against market volatility.</p></p></div><h3 class="adn-title" id="title-faq-3-6" role="button" aria-controls="content-faq-3-6" aria-expanded="false">What is DeFi?</h3><div class="adn-content" id="content-faq-3-6" role="region" aria-labelledby="title-faq-3-6" hidden><p><p>DeFi, or Decentralized Finance, refers to blockchain-based financial services that operate without traditional intermediaries. DeFi platforms offer lending, borrowing, trading, and earning interest using smart contracts.</p></p></div></div></div></div><div class="faqs-destacadas faqs-index" id="acordeon-4">
                <div class="wrapper faqs">
                    <div class="faqs-contents"><h2 class="font">Mining and Technology</h2><p><p>Cryptocurrency mining and the underlying technology can be complex for newcomers.
