<p>At the beginning of 2010, after nearly 3 years of full-timing in our RV, we bought <a href="https://roadslesstraveled.us/hunter44ds/" title="2008 Hunter 44DS sailboat named Groovy" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong>a sailboat</strong></a> and took our travels to the sea.  For the better part of the next 4 years, we cruised our 44′ sailboat <em>Groovy</em> up and down the entire 2,000 mile <a href="https://roadslesstraveled.us/pacific-mexico-destinations/" title="Pacific coast Mexico travel adventures and destinations" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong>Pacific coast of Mexico</strong></a>, spending the summertime hurricane seasons traveling in our trailer in the US while <em>Groovy</em> waited for us in Mexico.</p>
<p>We sailed from the US border to the Guatemala border, and visited almost every Mexican port in between, including the <a href="https://roadslesstraveled.us/sea-of-cortez-sailing-cruise-mexicos/" title="Sea of Cortez Mexico sailing adventure travel destinations" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong>Sea of Cortez</strong></a>.  We also <a href="https://roadslesstraveled.us/central-mexico-southern-mexico-travel-destinations/" title="Mexico travel adventures to Colonial Cities and Mayan Ruins" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>headed inland</strong></a> for some of our best adventures, taking public buses on long journeys to see Mexico&#8217;s magical colonial cities and visit the mind-blowing Mayan ruins way down south in the jungle.</p>

<p>In 2020 we put down roots once again and returned to living a conventional life in a stick-built home.  </p>
<p>In early 2021, after 7 months of being happy homebodies, we yearned for the thrill of life on the road.  We bought a truck camper for shorter duration adventures, however it wasn&#8217;t the right rig for us.</p>
<p>In 2022 we sold the camper and purchased a barely used <a href="https://roadslesstraveled.us/toy-hauler-life-whats-it-like-to-live-in-a-toy-hauler/" title="Toy Hauler Life – What’s  It Like to Live in a Toy Hauler?" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>Genesis Supreme 28CRT toy hauler</strong></a>. <p>During our summer months of travel in 2023 our wonderful 2016 Dodge Ram diesel truck died, costing us $6,600 in repairs to get back up and running again (it needed a new exhaust manifold and turbo).  The following May it began throwing the same error codes again, so we traded it in for a 2021 Toyota Tundra gas truck and in January 2025 we bought a 2024 Arctic Wolf 17CB travel trailer.</p>

<p>We are perpetually wandering off the well-worn paths of other travelers.</p>
<p>So, years ago, a friend sent me an email referencing Robert Frost&#8217;s poem <em>The Road Not Taken</em> after he read my post about <a title="Why go RVing fulltime?" href="https://roadslesstraveled.us/why-do-it/"><strong>why we are pursuing this travel dream</strong></a>. <p>The final lines of that famous poem, written so long ago, ring true for us today:</p>
<p style="padding-left: 33%;"><em>Two roads diverged in a wood<br />
